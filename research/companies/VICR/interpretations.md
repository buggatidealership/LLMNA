## 2026-05-20 — Initial thesis interpretations

### The "AVGO downstream beneficiary" framing was wrong

My initial framing of VICR as the "guaranteed asymmetric downstream beneficiary of Anthropic-Broadcom custom Si" anchored on the catalyst narrative without doing customer-level bottoms-up research. The bottoms-up work surfaces that:
- VICR lost the most important current-gen design (H100) to MPS
- VICR is replaced at one of top-2 hyperscalers on the highest-volume part
- The "guaranteed beat" thesis cannot hold when the customer relationship is contested

The actual thesis is binary on 2nd gen VPD adoption at NEXT-gen designs (Rubin, MI400, hyperscaler custom Si beyond current iterations). That's a real bull case but it's not a "downstream supplier" pattern — it's a "design-win event-driven" pattern.

### The math the bull case requires

Management projects $1B+ product revenue in 2026 (per Q4 2025 earnings call). Q1 2026 came in at $113.0M, implying $452M annualized run-rate. To hit $1B requires:
- Q2 2026: ~$200M+
- Q3 2026: ~$300M+
- Q4 2026: ~$400M+ (back-half loaded by 2nd gen VPD ramp)

The Q1 print of $113M does not visibly support that trajectory. The Q2 print (when it lands) is the first material falsifier. If Q2 doesn't show meaningful step-up (e.g., $130–150M range vs $200M+ needed), the $1B projection is on track to miss.

### The 2nd gen VPD lead customer is the most important unknown

VICR launched 2nd gen VPD in Q1 2026 for a "lead customer" they didn't name. The identity matters enormously:
- If it's a hyperscaler (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN) custom Si — high-volume potential
- If it's a frontier model provider (Anthropic, OpenAI) infrastructure — high-volume potential
- If it's NVIDIA Rubin — game-changing (would directly reverse the H100 design-out narrative)
- If it's AMD MI400 — material positive
- If it's a smaller AI chip startup (Cerebras, Groq, SambaNova) — lower volume but still legitimate

Public disclosure on the lead customer is the single highest-information catalyst available for VICR. Worth monitoring closely.

### The SemiAnalysis short thesis is worth respecting

Per the methodology, Dylan Patel / SemiAnalysis is a primary-tier source. The "Vicor Short Thesis" published by The Razor's Edge and the supporting analysis in the SemiAnalysis newsletter both make the same structural argument: VICR has been losing to MPS because MPS solved their footprint disadvantage at lower cost. That's not narrative — that's customer-level reality at H100 and top-2 hyperscalers.

The bull rebuttal: 2nd gen VPD at 5 A/mm² is technologically beyond what MPS can match in the current generation. If next-gen ASICs require that density, VICR wins. But "if" is doing a lot of work. The conservative interpretation: don't assume the 2nd gen VPD wins until customer disclosure.

### Recognition Stage 3 — the recent stock move complicates entry

VICR has already moved meaningfully on the AI power narrative (+18.6% on the most recent move alone). The story is known. Sell-side has coverage. Counter-thesis pieces are published. This is Stage 3, not Stage 1–2.

At Stage 3, the asymmetric setup requires the SPECIFIC outcome (design win) to materialize. Entering before that materializes = betting on a binary outcome at unfavorable odds (given the stock has already partially priced the bull case).

### What I'd want to see before entering

1. Public disclosure of 2nd gen VPD lead customer — material positive needed
2. Q2 2026 print showing revenue ≥ $150M with clear acceleration commentary
3. NVDA Rubin or AMD MI400 power architecture disclosure that requires VICR-class density
4. Any one of the above unblocks an entry; absence of all three means continue watching
