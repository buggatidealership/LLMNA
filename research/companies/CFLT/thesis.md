## TL;DR

Confluent (the commercial Kafka company) has **announced a proposed acquisition by IBM at $31.00 per share, expected to close mid-2026** per [Yahoo Finance Q1 CFLT earnings](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/cflt-q1-earnings-call-confluent-124716110.html). **This fundamentally changes the position thesis** — it's no longer a growth-equity decision but a merger-arb decision (cash-merger upside if deal closes, downside if deal breaks).

Pre-deal Q1 2025 metrics (most recent fully-disclosed): revenue $271.1M (+24.8% YoY), non-GAAP EPS $0.08 (+16.5% above consensus) per same source. Lowered FY revenue guide to $1.11B (from $1.12B) — cloud optimization headwind acknowledged.

**Key AI products** pre-deal: Flink (stream processing), Tableflow (Kafka topics ↔ Iceberg tables), strong enterprise data streaming for AI use cases. 340 net new customers in Q1 (3-year high) per same source.

**Position recommendation:** **DO NOT enter as growth thesis.** If considering as merger-arb, the question is "current price vs $31 vs probability of deal close." Out of scope for this OS (we don't trade merger-arb). Watchlist only — surfaces back to active research IF deal breaks.

## The business (pre-deal)

Commercial Kafka company — data streaming platform. Founders are original Kafka authors from LinkedIn. Customers: enterprises building real-time data pipelines.

Pre-deal product expansion: Flink for stream processing, Tableflow for unification with data lakes, Connectors for SaaS integrations.

## Why this matters for AI (pre-deal context)

Per `research/wiki/agentic-ai-enterprise.md`: agentic workflows need real-time data streaming (event-driven architectures). Kafka is the dominant open-source standard; CFLT was building the commercial business. The IBM acquisition consolidates this layer under IBM's enterprise sales motion.

For the user: if IBM closes the deal, CFLT's role in agentic AI infrastructure becomes part of IBM. If deal breaks, CFLT stays a standalone — and the standalone fundamentals (24.8% revenue growth, lowered FY guide) are mixed.

## Falsifiers (for the merger thesis specifically)
