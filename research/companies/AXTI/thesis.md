## Blind spots / what I may have missed

Per user instruction 2026-05-21:

1. **Specific FY 2027 capacity ramp curve.** "Doubling by 2027" is qualitative; the exact quarterly trajectory affects EPS modeling.

2. **Customer concentration in the $100M InP backlog.** Is it 80% Lumentum + Coherent, or diversified across 5-10 customers? Affects renewal risk.

3. **Indium pricing trajectory.** Indium is a critical input material; price spikes affect margins.

4. **China rare-earth / indium export policy.** AXT has US + China operations; geopolitical exposure.

5. **Substitute substrate technology risk.** Could gallium nitride or other compound semis displace InP for some applications? Technical question I cannot fully assess.

6. **The $632.5M capital raise dilution.** Issuing shares at recent high prices is constructive for AXT but dilutes existing holders.

7. **Working capital intensity of capacity buildout.** Growing 2-3x revenue requires significant working capital; cash flow visibility unclear.

8. **Insider transactions.** Are insiders selling into the rally?

9. **My ~10-20% earnings / 80-90% multiple decomposition is qualitative.** A more rigorous attribution would use the actual trailing EPS history and multiple compounding over time.

10. **Whether AXT will lose the "60-70% InP market share" position as Chinese competitors ramp.** Specifically referenced in `optical-interconnect-primer.md` as a watch item.

**Most important blind spot:** the specific quarterly EPS trajectory. My ~$0.30 FY 2026 EPS estimate is back-of-envelope. If actual FY 2026 EPS comes in materially higher ($0.50-0.60), the forward P/E compresses meaningfully without stock move and the bear case weakens. Worth verifying with actual Q2 2026 print.
