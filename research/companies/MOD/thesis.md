## Business

Modine plays at the **facility + rack cooling layer** of AI data centers — chillers (TurboChill 3+MW), CDUs (Airedale brand, 400kW-2MW+), dry coolers, AHUs, heat exchangers. NOT chip-level (Asetek/LiquidStack/CoolIT territory) and NOT server-level cold plates. Three segments through Q3 FY26:
1. Climate Solutions (HVAC Technologies + Heat Transfer Solutions + Data Centers) — growing fast, AI tailwind
2. Performance Technologies (auto/industrial thermal — legacy; being spun off into Gentherm reverse Morris Trust Q4 CY2026)
3. **Data Centers** — elevated to standalone third segment effective April 1, 2026 (Q4 FY26 first quarter reporting under new structure)

## Bypass routes / competitive substitutes (per Critical Rule #9)

For Modine's cooling layer specifically:
- **CDU sub-segment substitution risk:** HIGH — Vertiv, nVent, Schneider, Trane, Daikin, JCI Silent-Aire, AAON, CoolIT (post-Ecolab acquisition) all alternative suppliers. Deschutes open CDU spec lowers entry barriers per Dell'Oro.
- **Chiller sub-segment substitution risk:** LOW — TurboChill is purpose-built for AI DC free-cooling; legacy Trane/JCI chillers are HVAC-style, not AI-DC-optimized; this is Modine's defensible moat layer.
- **Alternative cooling topology workarounds:** immersion cooling (LiquidStack, GRC) — different topology, not direct Modine substitute at the facility layer.
- **End-customer adaptation:** hyperscalers can vertically integrate cooling design (some already have — Meta, MSFT have internal thermal engineering teams) but rely on Modine/Vertiv/etc. for physical equipment supply.
