## TL;DR

IREN Limited (formerly Iris Energy) is a pure power-asset play with massive scaling. **2,750 MW of Texas grid connections** (Childress 750MW + Sweetwater 1 1,400MW + Sweetwater 2 600MW) per [IREN IR](https://irisenergy.gcs-web.com/news-releases/news-release-details/iren-business-update). **Microsoft $9.7B cloud capacity deal signed** per [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/microsoft-signs-97bn-cloud-capacity-deal-with-iren/). 2026 targets: 480 MW AI Cloud capacity, 150,000 GPUs, **$3.7B annualized revenue run-rate by year-end** per same. Aschenbrenner $401M position per `research/signals/events/2026-05-21-aschenbrenner-q1-13f.md`.

## Key facts

- Q1 FY26 GPUs installed/on order: 150,000
- 2026 AI Cloud capacity target: 480 MW
- Year-end 2026 ARR target: $3.7B
- Texas grid connections: 2,750 MW total
- Microsoft contract: $9.7B
- APAC pipeline anchored by Australian opportunities

## Why this works

Per `research/wiki/power-for-ai-primer.md`: time-to-power is binding. IREN's 2,750 MW Texas grid connections were assembled over 3-7 years and cannot be replicated in time-to-power-sensitive AI buildout. Microsoft's $9.7B commitment validates the structural moat.

## Anti-fragility

WIN in S3 (power binds — IREN owns the power). Modest in S1/S2 (hosts whichever chip wins). LOSS in S4. Anti-fragility 2.5/5.

## Position recommendation

2-4% entry candidate. **Slightly preferred over CORZ** because:
- Larger contracted backlog (Microsoft $9.7B vs CoreWeave $8.7B at CORZ)
- More diversified geographic + customer pipeline
- More AI Cloud-direct revenue model (not just hosting)
- Better Aschenbrenner sizing ($401M vs $389M)

## Blind spots
- BTC mining business residual financial impact
- Capex requirements for 480 MW deployment
- GPU procurement risk (NVDA allocation)
- Australian regulatory environment for the APAC expansion
