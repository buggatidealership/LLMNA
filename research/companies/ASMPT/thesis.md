**Last updated:** 2026-05-25 (initial Phase 1 build)
**Tier:** Active candidate (Phase 1 surfaced — TCB market leader; entering hybrid bonding)
**Position target:** 2-4% if entered (user holds 0%)
**Anti-fragility:** 4/5 scenarios (wins on HBM TCB scaling + advanced packaging adoption across all 3 HBM vendors; loses if hybrid bonding transition compresses TCB share faster than thesis assumes)

**Foundation:** `research/wiki/advanced-packaging-primer.md` (built 2026-05-25), `research/wiki/hbm-primer.md`

## TL;DR

TCB (Thermo-Compression Bonding) market leader targeting **35-40% market share**. **2025 TCB revenue +146% YoY** (record) with FIREBIRD family deployed at **500+ TCB systems in mass production worldwide**. **TCB orders secured for 12-layer HBM4 + participation in 16-layer HBM4 development.** TCB TAM revised $760M 2025 → $1.6B 2028 (30% CAGR per ASMPT estimate). Entering hybrid bonding with first equipment delivered to logic customer Q3 2024 + two next-gen HB orders for HBM Q3 2025. Advanced packaging ~25% of total revenue with broader business diversification than BESI's hybrid-bonding concentration.

## Bull case

- TCB market leadership (35-40% share target) at current HBM3E/HBM4 12-Hi stacking volume
- 500+ deployed TCB systems = installed-base moat + service revenue annuity
- TCB TAM expansion $760M → $1.6B 2028 (30% CAGR) per ASMPT own estimate
- Successful entry into hybrid bonding with HBM customer orders Q3 2025 — captures both current TCB + next-gen hybrid bonding revenue
- Hong Kong listing accessibility for institutional capital (vs BESI's Amsterdam primary listing)
- Broader business diversification (25% advanced packaging) provides downside floor in cyclical downturn
- AI customer engagement deep across all HBM vendors

## Bear case

- BESI dominates hybrid bonding precision (<10nm) — ASMPT must catch up
- Hybrid bonding transition compresses TCB share over 5-7 years
- Customer concentration in HBM (3-vendor: SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron)
- ASML entering hybrid bonding (TrendForce March 2026) — additional competitive entrant
- Hong Kong listing geopolitical risk (US-China tariff overhang on Chinese-listed tech)
- Cyclical equipment supplier multiple compression risk (Cisco-1999 analog)
- Less-concentrated hybrid bonding bet = less asymmetric upside than BESI

## Base case

- TCB revenue continues +30-50% YoY through 2027 as HBM4 12-Hi scales
- Hybrid bonding revenue ramps modestly 2026-2028 ($50-150M annual)
- Advanced packaging segment grows from ~25% to ~40% of total revenue by 2028
- Total revenue grows 20-30% 2026-2028

## Falsifiers

1. **TCB market share drops below 30%** within 18 months → competitive moat eroding
2. **BESI captures meaningful TCB orders away from ASMPT** → market-share-shift confirmation
3. **HBM4 12-Hi adoption slows materially** → TCB TAM growth weakens
4. **Hybrid bonding transition accelerates faster than ASMPT can catch up to BESI** → secular share-loss
5. **Hong Kong listing tariff/sanctions overhang** materializes → access risk for international investors
6. **AMAT-BESI Kynex bundling locks ASMPT out of integrated D2W platform**
