## TL;DR

**#2 global 300mm silicon wafer supplier (~23-27% market share per orthogonal sources)** behind Shin-Etsu. Producing ~1.8M 300mm wafers/month across Japan-based fabs. Top-5 oligopoly (Shin-Etsu + SUMCO + GlobalWafers + Siltronic + SK Siltron) controls ~85% of global 300mm capacity per LinkedIn/SEMI. **Strategic pivot:** terminating 200mm wafer production at Miyazaki plant by late 2026 to shift entirely to high-end 300mm AI-grade wafers per [SEMI press release](https://www.semi.org/en/semi-press-release/semi-reports-2025-annual-worldwide-silicon-wafer-shipments-and-revenue-results) + market research aggregators.

**User's pattern observation 2026-05-25:** "Hasn't reached an all-time high yet... last all-time high was 2007... but now it's approaching slowly but steadily." Consistent with pre-GFC semi-cycle peak followed by ~17-year structural recovery now reactivating via AI-grade demand.

**Binding-constraint test (principle #26):** Wafers are foundational substrate for ALL advanced AI chips. 300mm AI-grade epitaxial wafers with <1nm surface roughness are STRUCTURALLY required for sub-3nm logic + HBM4 + advanced packaging — NOT a discretionary feature. Hybrid case: AI-grade 300mm = structural; legacy 200mm + commodity = cyclical. Same pattern as SNDK's NAND hybrid (AI-storage structural / consumer cyclical).

## Where Sumco sits in the AI stack

**TWO layers below the bonding-tech choice:**
- Layer 0 — silicon ingot + wafer (Sumco, Shin-Etsu)
- Layer 1 — fab/foundry (TSMC, Samsung Foundry, Intel Foundry)
- Layer 2 — front-end + back-end processing (KLA, AMAT, ASML for front-end; BESI/ASMPT for bonding; DISCO for grinding)
- Layer 3 — chip designers (NVDA, AMD, custom-Si)
- Layer 4 — system OEMs + hyperscalers

**Sumco is at the foundational substrate layer — every AI chip starts as a Sumco/Shin-Etsu wafer.** Even more upstream than DISCO (which is one layer below the bonding-tech choice).

## Apply principle #26 — binding-constraint test

**Hybrid case** (similar pattern to SNDK NAND):

| Sumco segment | Pre-AI framing | Binding-constraint test | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI-grade 300mm epitaxial wafers (2nm/3nm logic + HBM4) | Cyclical | Sub-3nm logic + HBM4 16-Hi require ultra-flat low-defect wafers; can't make AI chips without them | STRUCTURAL — newly binding |
| Commodity 300mm wafers (mature nodes) | Cyclical | Standard fab cycle; cyclical demand patterns | CYCLICAL — unchanged |
| 200mm wafers (legacy applications) | Cyclical | Mature; weakening demand; Sumco exiting | CYCLICAL — declining |

**Mix-shift thesis:** Sumco is actively transitioning from CYCLICAL legacy 200mm + commodity 300mm → STRUCTURAL AI-grade 300mm. The 200mm exit by late 2026 accelerates the mix shift. **The investable question is whether the structural-AI-grade mix grows fast enough to offset legacy decline + sustain re-rating.**
