# AGILITY (Agility Robotics, private) — Thesis stub

**Last updated:** 2026-05-24
**Tier:** Watchlist (private — IPO watch)
**Position target:** Cannot size while private; watch for IPO filing
**Anti-fragility:** 3/5 scenarios (humanoid-specific; concentration in warehouse/logistics use case)

## TL;DR
**The only Western humanoid earning real commercial revenue (as of April 2026 per Sacra).** Operating at GXO warehouse under paid RaaS model since mid-2024; moved 100K+ totes; **signed paying contracts with Toyota AND Mercado Libre**. RoboFab Salem facility capacity 10K units/year. Digit humanoid focused on warehouse / logistics workflows — narrower form-factor than general-purpose humanoid (Figure, Tesla, Apptronik) but with verified commercial pull. The Western humanoid bull case currently concentrates in this one private name.

## Bull case
- ONLY Western humanoid with verified productive commercial revenue (vs Tesla/Figure/Apptronik all pilot-stage)
- GXO RaaS contract since mid-2024 — multi-year proof of unit economics
- Paid contracts: Toyota, Mercado Libre (verified via Sacra April 2026)
- RoboFab Salem: 10K units/year capacity (verified)
- Focused-form-factor advantage: warehouse/logistics vs general-purpose attempt at everything
- Multi-narrative weak as private name but acquisition or IPO would surface anti-fragile humanoid exposure

## Bear case
- Private — no current accessibility for portfolio
- IPO timing + pricing uncertain
- Chinese humanoid (Unitree, UBTECH, AgiBot — 90% of global units) competing on price
- Apptronik + Jabil manufacturing partnership could close the production-scale gap
- Concentration in warehouse/logistics use case = vulnerable to warehouse-automation cycle

## Base case
- Continued GXO + Toyota + Mercado Libre deployment expansion through 2026
- IPO filing within 12-18 months (likely 2026-2027 window given $14B Series valuations across humanoid peers)
- If IPO surfaces, becomes highest-conviction Western humanoid pure-play

## Falsifiers
1. **GXO RaaS contract terminated OR reduced scope** → unit-economics moat breaks
2. **Toyota / Mercado Libre contracts don't expand** → commercial pull narrower than Sacra implies
3. **Chinese humanoid (Unitree, UBTECH) achieves Western warehouse qualification** at sub-$30K pricing → competitive bypass-route
4. **IPO priced significantly above $5B** → bubble-risk overhang ahead of operational proof
