# BESI (BE Semiconductor Industries — AMS:BESI / NASDAQ:BSEMY) — Thesis stub

**Last updated:** 2026-05-25 (initial Phase 1 build)
**Tier:** Active candidate (Phase 1 surfaced — hybrid bonding leader at structural choke point)
**Position target:** 2-4% if entered (user holds 0%)
**Anti-fragility:** 4/5 scenarios (wins on hybrid bonding adoption across all HBM vendors + logic + memory; loses if ASML entry compresses moat sooner than thesis assumes OR if HBM4E adoption delays)

**Foundation:** `research/wiki/advanced-packaging-primer.md` (built 2026-05-25), `research/wiki/hbm-primer.md`

## TL;DR

Dutch hybrid bonding equipment leader with **<10nm precision moat** — best-in-class for the next-gen Cu-to-Cu direct bonding required for HBM4E 16-Hi stacks + future 3D-IC packaging. **Hybrid bonding revenue projected €476M by 2026** = ~1/3 of total business per AInvest. **150+ cumulative orders from 18 customers** per March 2026 investor presentation. **Q4 backlog +105% YoY** driven by hybrid bonding demand. Partnership with Applied Materials on Kynex die-to-wafer system — first integrated D2W hybrid bonding platform.

## Bull case

- Hybrid bonding becomes mandatory for HBM4E 16-Hi stacks onwards — required physical precision (<10nm) only BESI delivers at high-volume currently
- TSMC CoWoS quadrupling to 130K wpm by late 2026 = 4x equipment demand growth potential at the back-end packaging layer (per `wiki/advanced-packaging-primer.md`)
- Process-of-record positions at leading logic + memory customers in 2025-2026 = decade-long stickiness per TSPA Semiconductor analysis
- AMAT partnership integrates BESI into front-end-to-back-end value chain — distribution moat compounds
- All 3 HBM vendors (Hynix, Samsung, Micron) evaluating hybrid bonding for HBM4 — competitive standardization
- First hybrid-bonded 16-high HBM4E stacks anticipated 2026 per BESI commentary

## Bear case

- **ASML entering hybrid bonding development** (TrendForce March 2026) — disruptive threat over 5-7 years. ASML lithography precision heritage translates partially to bonding
- Hybrid bonding adoption could slip past 2027 if HBM4E 16-Hi delays
- AMAT is BOTH partner AND potential competitor — Kynex co-development could shift to AMAT-owned IP
- ASMPT entering hybrid bonding with first HBM customer orders Q3 2025
- AMS:BESI European listing exposure (Dutch tax regime + EU regulatory)
- Multiple compression risk per Cisco-1999 analog (per `wiki/model-economics-primer.md`) — equipment supplier at peak-cycle pricing

## Base case

- Hybrid bonding revenue €476M 2026 → grows 25-40% CAGR through 2028 as HBM4E ramps
- Moat holds through 2030 (ASML catch-up cycle 5-7 years)
- Process-of-record positions secure at SK Hynix + Samsung + Micron + TSMC + Intel by 2027
- BESI total revenue grows 25-35% 2026-2028 as hybrid bonding becomes >50% of business

## Falsifiers

1. **ASML hybrid bonding equipment ships to a meaningful customer (HBM vendor) within 18 months** → BESI moat compression timeline accelerates materially
2. **HBM4E 16-Hi stack adoption pushed past 2028** → hybrid bonding demand timeline pushes out
3. **AMAT-BESI Kynex partnership terminates or restructures** → distribution channel risk
4. **AMD/NVDA/etc. announce shift to alternative bonding technology** for next-gen stacking
5. **BESI capacity expansion fails to meet order book** → execution risk on the +105% backlog
6. **HBM customer concentration risk** materializes (3-customer concentration in HBM vendors)
