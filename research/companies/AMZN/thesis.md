### Leg 2: Custom silicon (Trainium / Inferentia / Graviton)

Per [TIKR](https://www.tikr.com/blog/amazon-beat-q1-2026-earnings-on-every-line-the-bigger-story-is-the-chip-business-inside-aws):
- $20B annualized custom Si business
- Triple-digit YoY growth
- $225B+ Trainium revenue commitments
- Anthropic 5 GW Trainium capacity commitment
- OpenAI 2 GW Trainium commitment
- "Trainium could save tens of billions of dollars of capital expenditures per year and create several hundred basis points of operating margin advantage versus relying on third-party chips for inference"

Per `research/wiki/custom-silicon-primer.md`: AWS Trainium is the second-most-deployed custom silicon program (after Google TPU). Trainium 3 specs: 144 GB HBM3E, 4.9 TB/s per `research/wiki/hbm-primer.md`. **MRVL is design partner for Trainium** — also a derivative beneficiary.

### Leg 3: Anthropic equity stake + cloud-customer relationship

Amazon holds significant equity stake in Anthropic. Per `research/meta/private-tracker.md` and [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/30/google-amazon-ai-profits-anthropic-stake-bubble-earnings-2026/): Anthropic equity stake materially contributed to AMZN Q1 2026 reported earnings. With Anthropic Q2 2026 forecast $10.9B revenue + first operating profit (per `research/signals/triangulation.md`), this equity stake is increasingly valuable.

Plus: Anthropic is AWS's primary cloud customer. Anthropic's growth = AWS growth.

## Blind spots / what I may have missed

Per user instruction 2026-05-21:

1. **Specific Anthropic stake equity value disclosure.** AMZN holds Anthropic equity but the marked value (vs cost) is not consistently disclosed.

2. **AWS regional revenue mix.** US vs international growth dynamics affect both pricing and regulatory exposure.

3. **Retail business margins.** Amazon retail historically operated at thin margins; the 13.1% record op margin is heavily AWS-weighted. Retail trajectory matters less for AI thesis but affects total returns.

4. **AWS capex sustainability questions.** $59.3B annualized capex is enormous. Long-term ROI vs depreciation schedules matters.

5. **Competition from Microsoft Azure + Google Cloud.** AWS growth +28% but Azure and Google Cloud are growing faster off smaller bases. Share dynamics over time.

6. **Trainium customer concentration beyond Anthropic + OpenAI.** Are there other major Trainium customers? Or is it 2 customers carrying most of the $225B+ commits?

7. **Bedrock revenue specifically.** Bedrock is AWS's managed AI platform. Revenue split between Bedrock (managed) and Trainium-rental (compute-only) not fully disclosed.

8. **Anti-trust / regulatory risk on AWS.** Some calls for break-up exist; less urgent than 2-3 years ago but persistent.

9. **Stock recent valuation.** I haven't pulled current forward P/E. AMZN traditionally trades at premium given growth profile.

10. **Operational complexity of running both AWS + retail + Anthropic stake.** Management bandwidth, capital allocation discipline.

**Most important blind spot:** the ROI sustainability of the $59.3B capex. If incremental AWS revenue per dollar of capex is declining, the bull case weakens even with strong absolute revenue growth.
