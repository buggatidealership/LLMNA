## TL;DR
Public-market warehouse RaaS exemplar. Walmart anchor + acquired Walmart's internal robotics arm Jan 2025 (proprietary sortation IP). $300M institutional raise Oct 2025 funding 2nd US plant + regional service centers. Customer concentration risk on Walmart is acute — diversification proof needed to expand thesis confidence.

## Bull case
- Walmart anchor + sortation IP acquisition Jan 2025 = vertically-integrated competitive moat
- $300M raise Oct 2025 funding 2nd US manufacturing plant + regional service centers
- AMR/AGV market expected to be biggest by 2028 at ~30% CAGR
- Warehouse robotics broader market: $7-15B 2025 at 17-20% CAGR
- Per-pick / per-hour RaaS economics shift unit-of-sale frame favorably (recurring revenue)

## Bear case
- Walmart customer concentration risk acute — diversification proof needed
- AutoStore + Locus + Berkshire Grey + Amazon-in-house competing intensely
- AMZN does its own warehouse robotics (Amazon Robotics + Covariant acquisition) — biggest potential customer is in-house
- RaaS economics need to prove out over multi-year deployment cycles before sizing conviction
- Pre-profitability per recent earnings; cash burn vs ramp uncertain

## Base case
- Walmart deployment continues; 1-2 large secondary customers signed within 12-18 months
- $300M raise + 2nd plant enables ramp through 2027
- Margin path to profitability becomes visible in 2026-2027 earnings

## Falsifiers
1. **Walmart slows deployment OR shifts to in-house** → core thesis breaks
2. **No 2nd anchor customer signed within 18 months** → concentration risk doesn't resolve
3. **Margin path stays negative through 2027** → RaaS economics thesis fails
4. **Berkshire Grey + Locus + Geek+ consolidate share** → competitive intensity caps SYM growth
