## TL;DR
**The strongest single-name answer to "universal vision supplier across all Physical AI sub-domains."** Sony Semiconductor Solutions holds 43-64% global CMOS image sensor (CIS) market share (different sources, dominant range). CIS is required in mobile + automotive + industrial + medical + AR/VR + security + AI-RAN edge cameras + robotics — every Physical AI device class. Sony multi-narrative score 5/6 sub-domains under Physical AI lens (per `wiki/physical-ai-primer.md`). The 6-domain breakdown maps to robotics + AVs + industrial automation + digital twin (indirect via sensor feed) + AI-RAN + edge devices.

## Bull case
- **Dominant CIS market share** — 43.4% (one source) to 63.6% (another) globally; Sony + Samsung + GalaxyCore collectively ~62%
- **Multi-segment exposure**: Mobile (64% of CIS market — Sony LYT-828 50MP mass production Aug 2025); Automotive (9.4% CAGR — Sony IMX828 launched Oct 2025 with built-in MIPI APHY interface for ADAS); Industrial inspection; Medical (surgical robotics endoscopes); AR/VR; Security
- **Total CIS market**: $24.58B 2025 → $34.52B 2030 (7.12% CAGR overall — auto faster)
- **Physical AI universal-supplier dynamic**: vision is in every Physical AI device; Sony captures share independent of which sub-domain dominates
- **Sony Group conglomerate downside floor**: Gaming (PlayStation), Music, Pictures provide non-Physical-AI earnings stability

## Bear case
- Samsung CIS competition (especially in mobile flagship and select auto programs)
- GalaxyCore (Chinese CIS) gaining share at low-end
- Sony Group is a conglomerate — Sony Semiconductor Solutions is one segment, not the whole company; investing in 6758.T captures the CIS thesis dilutively (~20% of group revenue est)
- Mobile CIS pricing pressure (smartphone ASPs flat)
- CIS overall market grows only ~7% CAGR — multi-narrative benefit needs to play out via segment-mix shift toward higher-growth auto + industrial

## Base case
- Sony CIS share holds 45-55% range; auto segment grows 10%+ CAGR offsetting mobile flatness
- 2025-2030 segment-mix shifts toward higher-growth auto + industrial + medical
- The Sony Group conglomerate frame remains the structural negative for pure-play CIS exposure

## Falsifiers
1. **CIS market share drops below 35% globally within 24 months** → universal-supplier thesis weakens at vision layer
2. **Samsung captures meaningful share in automotive ADAS CIS** (currently Sony dominant) → auto-segment growth thesis weakens
3. **Sony Group segment reporting shows CIS revenue contracting YoY** for 2+ consecutive quarters → structural negative
4. **Chinese CIS suppliers (GalaxyCore, OmniVision) reach Western OEM qualification** in auto + industrial → bypass route validated; pricing power compresses
5. **Sony Group ownership structure changes** (e.g., Sony Semi spinout — could be positive OR negative depending on pricing)
