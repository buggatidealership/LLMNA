## TL;DR

Ibiden (TSE: 4062) is one of the dominant FC-BGA + EMIB-T advanced-packaging substrate suppliers globally (~5-firm oligopoly with ~74% combined market share per agent research [Digitimes T3](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20260430VL217/embedded-substrate-unimicron-semco-ibiden.html)). Ibiden is the primary Japan-based supplier of EMIB-T substrate to Intel's advanced packaging line that hyperscalers (Google v8e "Humufish" H2 2027; Amazon EMIB discussions per [Tom's Hardware T2](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/semiconductors/intel-reportedly-in-talks-with-google-and-amazon-over-advanced-packaging)) are adopting as TSMC-CoWoS-bypass for custom AI ASICs.

**The structural signal**: Ibiden announced ¥500B capex plan (¥220B first phase at Gama Plant Cell6) for FY26-28, operations starting 2027 / full mass production 2028 per [Globe and Mail relay of Ibiden IR T1](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/investing/markets/stocks/IBIDF/pressreleases/10864/ibiden-to-invest-yen500-billion-in-expanding-ic-package-substrate-capacity-for-ai-and-high-performance-servers/). Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan confirmed substrate supply is "extremely tight" and customers prepaying billions per Tom's Hardware T2. Per [TrendForce T3](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/05/20/news-intel-says-emib-customers-back-substrate-prepayments-4-taiwan-and-2-japan-suppliers-reportedly-seek-commitments/): customers collectively co-funded ~50% of capex at top 4 substrate suppliers.

**The customer co-funded capex pattern** is the strongest possible demand-pull signal — structurally similar to MOD $4B Airedale + SNDK $42B NBM customer commitments. Customer pre-funding capex = revealed demand conviction, not just expressed.

## Top-down framework application (Principle #33 — 2nd application)

End-task: AI accelerator advanced packaging at scale (custom ASICs for hyperscalers via EMIB-T bypass-route to TSMC CoWoS)

Capability stack:
- Die-to-die high-bandwidth interconnect → EMIB bridge die + substrate
- Signal integrity at 10+ Gbps per pin → embedded silicon caps + advanced substrate
- Power delivery → through-silicon vias + EMIB-T bridges
- Thermal management → substrate-level heat spreading

Binding constraint: **EMIB-T substrate capacity** — explicitly named "extremely tight" by Intel CEO; customer co-funding pattern = textbook revealed-demand-conviction signal.

**Competition handling (Principle #33 refined criteria 2026-05-28):** LIMITED. Five firms ~74% global FC-BGA share. Customer co-funding creates switching costs (customers who prepay Ibiden cannot easily redirect capital to a new supplier). Alternative suppliers (Shinko, Unimicron, AT&S) all similarly constrained per Intel disclosure. Bypass routes exist but all bypass routes are building toward the SAME shortage — no easy bypass with capacity available.

**Verdict: TIER S candidate** per refined criteria — passes binding-constraint test + competition is LIMITED (oligopoly + customer co-funding moat).
