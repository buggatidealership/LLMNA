# ISRG (Intuitive Surgical) — Thesis stub

**Last updated:** 2026-05-24
**Tier:** Watchlist (Phase 3 surfaced; deepest moat in robotics universe)
**Position target:** TBD — sizing-matrix tomorrow
**Anti-fragility:** 4/5 scenarios (surgical-specific limits multi-narrative; deep moat compensates)

## TL;DR
Most-verified-strong fundamentals in the robotics watchlist. ISRG controls 70%+ of global surgical procedure share; 11K+ installed da Vinci base; $4B 2025 recurring revenue; Q3 2025 revenue $2.51B (+23% YoY); da Vinci procedures +17% 2024; **Ion procedures +52%** (lung — multi-product growth verified). Hugo competitive entry from Medtronic (April 2025 FDA submission, 98.5% clinical success) is real BUT ISRG installed-base switching cost moat is deepest in robotics universe.

## Bull case
- 70%+ global procedure share (Grand View 2025)
- 11,000+ da Vinci systems installed (+13% YoY per Q3 2025 8-K)
- $4B 2025 recurring revenue (instruments + accessories + services); recurring revenue base creates compounding
- Multi-product growth: da Vinci +17% 2024 + Ion +52% in 2025 = NOT a one-product story anymore
- 5-10 year FDA regulatory pathway for competitors = built-in time moat
- Surgeon training + procedure-coding + installed-base switching cost compound
- da Vinci 5 next-gen launch underway (Phase 4 verification needed for cadence)

## Bear case
- Medtronic Hugo: April 2025 FDA submission; IDE clinical studies met endpoints in urology + gynecology + hernia; 98.5% clinical success rate. Real competitive entry.
- Stryker Mako gaining in orthopedic specialty (Care Hospitals Hyderabad deployment July 2025)
- J&J Ottava + CMR Surgical pipeline competition
- Asia-Pacific growing fastest (9.88% CAGR) but ISRG penetration uneven
- Multiple still elevated vs broader market — sensitive to medical-device-sector re-rating

## Base case
- da Vinci installed base continues compounding at 10-15%/year
- Ion procedure growth sustains 30-50% range
- Hugo competitive pressure shows up in 2027 new-system bookings but takes years to dent installed-base share
- ISRG retains 60%+ procedure share through 2030

## Falsifiers
1. **Hugo achieves >5% US new-system market share within 24 months of full launch** → competitive moat eroding faster than expected
2. **ISRG da Vinci procedure growth slows below 10%/year** → installed-base compounding thesis breaks
3. **Ion procedure growth slows below 20%/year** → multi-product diversification thesis weakens
4. **Stryker Mako captures >10% of joint-replacement procedures** in major US health systems → orthopedic-specialty erosion
