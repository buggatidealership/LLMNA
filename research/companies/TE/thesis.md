## The business

T1 Energy (NYSE: TE), formerly FREYR Battery, pivoted business model in late 2024 from battery manufacturing to US-domestic solar manufacturing. Per [Bitget overview](https://www.bitget.com/wiki/t1-energy-stock):
- US-based solar cell + module manufacturer
- Primary asset: G1_Dallas (Texas) photovoltaic module facility, 5 GW nameplate capacity
- Under construction: G2_Austin solar cell fab, 2.1 GW first phase, target late 2026 production

## Why this matters for AI

Per `research/wiki/power-for-ai-primer.md`:
- US datacenter power demand: 75.8 GW (2026) → 134 GW (2030)
- Supply deficit: 3-5 GW/year structural
- Clean energy mandates from hyperscalers (Microsoft net-zero by 2030; Google 24/7 carbon-free)

Solar is one of the ways hyperscalers meet clean-power requirements alongside nuclear PPAs. T1 Energy as domestic solar mfg benefits from:
1. Hyperscaler clean-power demand
2. US tariff barrier vs Chinese solar imports
3. IRA Section 45X production tax credits

2. **Stock recent action specifically.** Beyond the +14% in 30 days per Tickeron, I don't have full price action context. Could be already-priced or under-priced.

5. **G2 Austin construction risk.** Construction projects regularly slip. 6-12 month delays would push first revenue from G2 into 2027.

6. **Chinese tariff trajectory.** Tariffs on Chinese solar are favorable to T1 but could change with trade deals or be modified.

7. **Inventory / supply chain risks.** Solar manufacturing requires polysilicon (Corning's Hemlock supplies some), wafers, glass, frames. Any input shortage hits T1 disproportionately as a smaller player.

8. **Competitive moves from First Solar (FSLR), SunPower, others.** US domestic solar has multiple competitors with deeper resources.

9. **The "battery legacy" risk.** T1 still has some battery assets/obligations from FREYR. Could be drag on financials.

10. **Aschenbrenner's specific entry price + thesis.** I assumed he's positioned for the clean-power-for-AI thesis; could be a different rationale (e.g., 45X PTC arbitrage, takeout target).

**Most important blind spot:** Trump-admin IRA policy trajectory. This is the single most important variable for T1 — and I cannot predict it. The thesis lives or dies on policy. Position sizing should reflect this binary risk.
