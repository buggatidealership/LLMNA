## TL;DR
3/4 gate-stack on the medical-AI evaluation but **LABEL: distribution-moat + AI overlay, NOT Type-A AI-execution.** REiLI AI imaging platform FDA + CE cleared algorithms; embedded in Synapse PACS install base. Healthcare segment ¥975.1bn FY2024 (+5%) per T1 Fujifilm IR. The AI is a feature uplift on equipment sales, not a standalone revenue driver. **Conglomerate dilution: Healthcare is one segment of a multi-segment Fujifilm.**

## Why Fujifilm is Type-B (overlay, not execution)
- AI is bundled into Synapse PACS subscription (no discrete AI revenue line)
- Moat is the Synapse install base + Japanese healthcare distribution, not the REiLI algorithm itself
- Foundation-model imaging AI commoditizes the algorithm layer; what survives is the install base + regulatory clearances + Japanese-population data spine
- Same archetype as GE Healthcare / Siemens Healthineers / Philips — AI-adjacent imaging incumbent

## Bull case (P~30%, my model)
- REiLI international expansion (already FDA + CE cleared) into US/EU radiology
- Synapse Cosmos enterprise-AI platform sells as feature uplift across install base
- Conglomerate SOTP rerate if Healthcare segment AI-content becomes recognized

## Bear case (P~30%, my model)
- GE/SHL/PHG embed competing AI at scale on their dominant install bases
- Conglomerate discount widens; AI overlay never breaks out as discrete thesis
- Foundation-model PACS-AI from cloud providers (Google, Microsoft) commoditizes

## Base case (P~40%, my model)
- Steady healthcare segment compounding; AI is feature add not revenue driver; range-bound on multi-segment dynamics

## Falsifiers
1. Healthcare segment growth deceleration below mid-single digits
2. REiLI loses a major IDN contract to GE/SHL/PHG
3. AI revenue continues bundled with no discrete disclosure through 2027

## Exposure to causal chains
- Aging population + healthcare imaging volumes
- Japanese healthcare AI reimbursement expansion (Olympus EndoBRAIN +60点 precedent)
