## TL;DR

**User-articulated thesis 2026-05-25 verified:** *"if DISCO wins across the entire chain, then you would logically rather own that company that wins regardless of who wins at the top."* DISCO sits ONE LAYER BELOW the BESI vs ASMPT bonding-tech choice — every wafer needs grinding/polishing/dicing BEFORE any bonding step. **70-80% global market share** in dicing/grinding/polishing equipment per Goldman Sachs + JP Morgan analyst reports. **4x larger than closest competitor** (Tokyo Seimitsu/Accretech ~8-9% share). Customers: TSMC + Samsung + SK Hynix + all major power semi (SiC/GaN). **Consumables 20-25% of revenue** = recurring high-margin annuity scaling with installed base. SemiAnalysis explicitly compares DISCO to KLA-Corporation — same choke-point structural profile.

## Bull case

- **70-80% market share with 4x lead over closest competitor** — comparable to KLA-Corporation pricing-power profile per SemiAnalysis
- **Picks-and-shovels at the layer BELOW bonding-tech choice** — wins regardless of TCB vs hybrid bonding outcome; wins on EVERY future packaging iteration that requires wafer prep
- **Consumables 20-25% of revenue annuity** scaling with installed base — every BESI hybrid-bonded wafer + every ASMPT TCB-bonded wafer needs DISCO blades + grinding wheels first
- Customers include TSMC (CoWoS quadrupling), Samsung, SK Hynix (HBM4 ramp), all power semi fabs (SiC + GaN)
- Laser saw shipments >4,000 units Feb 2026 (3x growth since 2020) — successfully transitioning to advanced Stealth Dicing + KABRA laser slicing for SiC + GaN + advanced packaging
- High ROE + cash-rich balance sheet
- Premium tier specialty: SiC power devices + HBM4 advanced packaging (per MatrixBCG)
- Anti-fragility 5/5 — wins across virtually every scenario tied to advanced packaging or power semi growth

## Bear case

- **Concentration risk on TSMC/Samsung/SK Hynix triumvirate** — same end customers as BESI/ASMPT/HYNIX. If all slow, DISCO slows
- **Plasma dicing displacement risk** — emerging alternative to mechanical dicing; DISCO is investing in hybrid laser-mechanical platforms to defend
- **GL Tech (Chinese government-backed)** flagged as biggest long-term displacement threat per MatrixBCG; multi-year time horizon
- **Lower per-catalyst leverage** vs concentrated bets — HBM4E 16-Hi adoption benefits BESI proportionally MORE than DISCO; DISCO is structural-safety not catalyst-asymmetry
- Mature business cyclical to capex cycles — equipment + new-machine consumables affected by hyperscaler capex compression
- TYO listing adds to JPY concentration (alongside Murata, potentially HDS, Nabtesco if added)
- Cisco-1999 multiple compression risk applies (equipment supplier at peak cycle pricing)

## Base case

- Advanced packaging cycle continues through 2027-2028 (TSMC CoWoS sold out through 2027)
- DISCO grinding/polishing/dicing revenue grows 20-30% 2026-2028 as TSMC quadruples capacity
- Consumables annuity grows 15-20% with installed base expansion
- 70-80% market share holds through 2028 (Chinese GL Tech catch-up multi-year)
- Total revenue +20-30% 2026-2027

## Falsifiers

1. **DISCO market share drops below 60%** within 24 months → competitive moat eroding (Plasma dicing OR GL Tech displacement)
2. **Plasma dicing wins meaningful share at major hyperscaler design wins** → mechanical dicing moat compresses
3. **Consumables revenue % declines below 18%** → installed-base annuity eroding (replacement/refurbishment cycle accelerating)
4. **TSMC/Samsung/Hynix capex collectively guides DOWN materially** → demand-side concentration risk materializes
5. **AMAT or ASML acquires Accretech or GL Tech** → competitive consolidation tilts against DISCO
6. **Hybrid laser-mechanical platforms fail customer qualification** → DISCO's defensive technology investment doesn't materialize
