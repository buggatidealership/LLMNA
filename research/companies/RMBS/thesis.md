**Last updated:** 2026-05-21

## TL;DR

Rambus is the memory IP licensor + memory interface chip maker. Per `research/wiki/hbm-primer.md` §7 Tier 3 (HBM bypass routes): RMBS is a candidate for the memory IP angle. DDR5 RCD chips at up to 8000 MT/s; DDR6 roadmap to 18,000+ MT/s; CXL evolution per [Rambus product pages](https://www.rambus.com/memory-interface-chips/). Reported gross margins historically above 70%.

MRDIMM revenue ramp expected late 2026 → 2027 per [Rambus Q1 2026 earnings via Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/27/rambus-rmbs-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/). $600M TAM opportunity fully realized by 2028 per same.

## Why this works (but moderately)

Memory IP + interface chips benefit from every server platform refresh. Server unit growth from AI tailwind + MRDIMM adoption at hyperscalers = structural revenue.

BUT: RMBS is a smaller business compared to AVGO/MRVL. The MRDIMM ramp is anchored to Intel and AMD CPU platform launches — timing dependent on those companies.

## Position recommendation

1-2% entry candidate. Lower priority than direct AI plays. Memory IP is a thinner moat than direct compute or substrate.

## Blind spots
- Intel/AMD platform launch timing
- Specific RMBS share of CXL controller market vs Astera Labs (ALAB)
- Lawsuit / IP licensing one-time revenue volatility
- Stock recent price action
