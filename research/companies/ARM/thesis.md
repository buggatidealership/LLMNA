## The business — three independent growth vectors

ARM is no longer "just an IP licensor" (correcting prior framing). The business has THREE legs:

**Leg 1: IP licensing (the legacy moat)**
- Royalties: 5-7% of chip ASP base rate per [FinancialContent deep-dive](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-3-25-the-invisible-architect-a-2026-deep-dive-into-arm-holdings-nasdaq-arm)
- Armv9 chips command ~2× the royalty rate of v8 per same source
- Royalty revenue grew 11% Q4 FY26 YoY; data center royalties DOUBLED YoY for two consecutive years per Q4 FY26 results
- License revenue +29% Q4 YoY (forward-looking signal)

**Leg 2: Compute Subsystems (CSS) — the hyperscaler enabler**
- Pre-validated subsystem designs that hyperscalers integrate into their custom silicon
- Reduces hyperscaler chip TTM by 18 months per [FinancialContent](https://markets.financialcontent.com/stocks/article/finterra-2026-3-25-the-invisible-architect-a-2026-deep-dive-into-arm-holdings-nasdaq-arm)
- Direct enabler of AWS Graviton, Google Axion, Microsoft Cobalt, NVDA Vera
- 50% of hyperscaler CPU compute share per [DCD coverage](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/arm-posts-recording-breaking-revenue-for-full-year-and-q4-26-with-its-share-of-hyperscaler-cpu-compute-hitting-50/)

**Leg 3: AGI CPU (the new growth vector — user's correction validated)**
- **Launched March 2026** per [BigGo Q4 FY26](https://finance.biggo.com/news/US_ARM_2026-05-06)
- ARM's own AI CPU product (not just IP)
- **Demand doubled to $2B booked across FY27-28** per same source
- First production revenue Q4 FY27 (early 2027 calendar)
- Supply chain capacity is the binding constraint, not demand
- Long-term target: $15B AGI CPU revenue + $10B IP = $25B by FY2031

**Key customer wins disclosed Q4 FY26** per BigGo + [Futurum analysis](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/arm-q4-fy-2026-agentic-cpu-shift-boosts-demand-outlook/):
- Google TPU 8t and 8i paired with Arm Axion CPUs (80% performance improvement over x86)
- AWS Graviton and Trainium expansion
- Microsoft Cobalt
- NVIDIA Vera CPU
- Plus AGI CPU direct customers (unnamed; $2B booked)

## D1-D5 scoring

**D1. Structural relevance & displacement risk: HIGH at IP layer; MEDIUM at AGI CPU layer**
- IP layer: Layer 0 at CPU IP for mobile (95%+ share) + hyperscaler datacenter (50% share growing)
- AGI CPU layer: new entrant with $2B booked demand but unproven at scale
- **Displacement risk SHORT-TERM (1-3 years): LOW** — RISC-V penetration is in IoT/simple devices, not server-class
- **Displacement risk MEDIUM-TERM (3-5 years): MEDIUM** — RISC-V at 25% with Qualcomm-Ventana signal; Apple in-house path
- **Long-term physical-law moat: real but not infinite** — instruction set transitions are decade-long but they DO happen

**D2. Chokepoint severity: HIGH at CPU IP layer**
- Magnitude: $4.92B FY26 → $25B FY31 target = ~5× growth over 5 years
- Duration: 3-5+ years (CPU instruction set transitions slow)
- Pricing power: HIGH (Armv9 doubled royalty rate; CSS premium pricing)
- AGI CPU adds new chokepoint at AI CPU sub-layer

**D2.5 Proximity to bottleneck (per new dimension):**
- **Layer 0 at CPU IP bottleneck** (50% hyperscaler share = empirical Layer-0 positioning)
- **Layer 0 at AGI CPU (own product)** — new dimension
- **Layer 1 at agentic-AI CPU bottleneck** (body-of-agent per user framing; ARM enables most ARM-based AI servers)
- **Layer 0 at mobile CPU IP** (dominant, ~95% share)
- **Multi-bottleneck positioning across 4+ sub-layers** = comparable to ALAB's optionality
- BUT: similar to ALAB, Layer 0 positioning = customer relationships matter critically (Apple, hyperscalers, Qualcomm)

**D3. Competitive position: STRONG-DOMINANT with structural pressures**
- Dominant in mobile (~95%+)
- 50% hyperscaler share, growing
- AGI CPU is new entrant — competitive position TBD at scale
- **Structural competitive pressures:** Qualcomm legal loss, Ventana RISC-V acquisition, Apple custom path
- Net: STRONG TIER-1 IP but not immune to long-term competitive evolution

**D4. Mismodeling + rerating-arc position: LATE-ARC / structural with AGI CPU as fresh leg**
- Stock has doubled in 2026 per [24/7 Wall St](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/19/arm-holdings-has-doubled-in-2026-is-it-outperforming-other-chip-ip-stocks-like-synopsys-cadence-and-qualcomm/)

- BUT AGI CPU at anchor signal stage — multi-leg rerating possible if production ramps
- Forward P/E 132x = prices in ~38% CAGR through FY2031
- Mismodeling: AGI CPU upside vs RISC-V downside — both could be under-modeled in opposite directions

**My net independent view:**

ARM is a structural multi-vector CPU compounding story with one genuinely new product growth leg (AGI CPU) plus established IP business at 50% hyperscaler share. The "body of agent" framing per user is accurate — ARM enables most ARM-based AI server CPUs, and AGI CPU directly competes with AMD/Intel/NVDA Vera at the AI CPU layer.

**But the valuation prices in success** of all three vectors continuing. 132x forward P/E is rich even for the growth profile. Multi-vector positioning gives ARM downside-protection optionality (one vector fails, others continue) but doesn't justify the multiple if BOTH RISC-V threat materializes AND AGI CPU disappoints.

**The asymmetric setup is real BUT bounded by multiple compression risk.**
