## TL;DR

Hubbell is a US electrical infrastructure company with **dual exposure to datacenter buildout AND utility T&D (transmission & distribution) growth driven by AI load**. Q1 2026: revenue $1.52B (+11% net, +8.2% organic), adjusted EPS $3.93 (+16% YoY), beating consensus per [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/hubbell-q1-2026-slides-earnings-beat-raised-outlook-as-shares-fall-6-93CH-4650658).

**Electrical Solutions segment growing double-digit organically driven by datacenter + light industrial.** Utility T&D markets "remain strong, with load growth driving strength in transmission and substation markets" per [AOL/Motley Fool transcript](https://www.aol.com/articles/hubbell-hubb-q1-2026-earnings-163810463.html). FY2026 guide raised: adjusted EPS $19.30-19.85.

**Position recommendation:** 1-3% if entered. Narrower AI exposure than VRT or ETN — Hubbell is more utility-T&D-weighted. Best understood as a "grid load growth from AI" play rather than pure datacenter.

## The business

Per [Q1 2026 8-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000048898/000162828026028600/exhibit991_04302026.htm):
- **Utility Solutions** — T&D equipment, smart meters, grid hardware (sells to utilities; benefits from AI-driven load growth)
- **Electrical Solutions** — datacenter components, light industrial, lighting (more direct AI exposure)

## Why this matters for AI

Per `research/wiki/power-for-ai-primer.md`: hyperscaler AI load is forcing utilities to add transmission capacity, substations, and distribution upgrades. HUBB sells the equipment for those upgrades. Less direct than Eaton's in-datacenter products, more leverage to the utility side of the buildout.

The Electrical Solutions double-digit organic growth + datacenter call-out (per AOL/Motley Fool) is the direct AI signal. Utility T&D load growth is the indirect signal.
