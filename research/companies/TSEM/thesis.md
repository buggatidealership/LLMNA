## The business

Tower Semiconductor (NASDAQ: TSEM, also TASE) is an Israeli specialty foundry. NOT a leading-edge logic foundry (TSMC's space). Instead: specialty processes for analog, RF, power, MEMS, sensors, and — critically — **silicon photonics**.

Per `wiki/optical-interconnect-primer.md`: silicon photonics is the foundation of the CPO (co-packaged optics) transition. Specialty foundries like Tower are where silicon photonics chips are manufactured.

Tower's silicon photonics platform (PH18) is one of the most-deployed silicon photonics processes globally. Customers include hyperscalers + optical companies + AI infrastructure providers.

## Silicon photonics: the core thesis

Per `wiki/optical-interconnect-primer.md`:
- CPO transition 2026-2030; large-scale 3-5 years out
- NVDA Spectrum-X CPO targets 3.2T per stack 2H 2026
- Lumentum + Coherent receive NVDA $2B investment for CPO supply

Tower's role: foundry for the silicon photonics chips that go INTO CPO modules. Customers include:
- Coherent (COHR) per the 400 Gbps/lane demonstration
- Lumentum (LITE) likely (per Tower's PH18 platform deployment)
- Hyperscaler silicon photonics designs (specifics not publicly disclosed)

The $1.3B 2027 contract book + $290M customer prepayments means Tower has VISIBLE forward demand. This is rare in foundry — typically demand is back-end-loaded and uncertain.

6. **Israel manufacturing geopolitical risk.** Tower has fabs in Israel. Regional events affect.

7. **Stock recent action.** I have not pulled current valuation multiple post-Q1 print.

8. **CPO competition from other foundries.** TSMC has its own silicon photonics offering. GlobalFoundries also. Tower's specific competitive position is not fully verified.

9. **The "all about silicon photonics now" framing.** SemiconAlpha titled their Q1 review this way. Is the rest of the business actually declining or just slower-growing?

10. **2027 revenue contract specifics.** "Silicon photonics contracts for $1.3 billion for 2027" — is this CONTRACTED (firm) or COMMITTED capacity (with optional drawdown)? Material difference.

**Most important blind spot:** customer concentration in the $1.3B 2027 number. If this is 1-2 customers, renewal risk is real. If diversified across 4-5 customers, much more durable.
