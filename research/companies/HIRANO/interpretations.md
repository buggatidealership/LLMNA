## On the "Lithography-Tier Equivalent" Framing

(interpretation) The "Hirano = lithography-tier equivalent within MLCC manufacturing stack" framing — most technically demanding, narrowest competitive set, highest switching-cost step — is intellectually compelling but **probably overstated**. The lithography analogy works because ASML has literal regulatory + technical monopoly at EUV; Hirano has no regulatory protection and the "monopoly" at ultra-thin end is based purely on accumulated process knowledge + machine qualification. This is a moat, but a TRADE-SECRETS-AND-QUALIFICATION MOAT rather than IP-protected-patent moat. **Stress test: could a Chinese equipment maker, given 5 years + sufficient reverse engineering, replicate Hirano's ultra-thin forming capability? Probably yes at lab scale; probably not within 24-36 month thesis window at production scale with qualification at Murata/TDK.** This makes 24-36 month thesis sound even if 5-10 year thesis has genuine competitive risk.
