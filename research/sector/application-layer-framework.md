## 1. The spine — the narrative mis-specifies the unit of disruption

The circulating frame ("death of SaaS → death of the SIer") is directionally alive but wrong at the unit level. The evidence supports a re-specification (research-verified 2026-07-07, step-0 artifact):

> **It is not "SaaS vs agents." It is per-seat-flat pricing vs usage/outcome pricing. It is not "SIer vs AI." It is 人月 (man-month) billing vs outcome billing.**

- Per-seat pricing IS structurally impaired: seat-based median NRR 95% (below the 100% breakeven) vs usage-based 108% (T2/T3 benchmark); ETR: 59% of shortfall-hit orgs cut software subscriptions FIRST vs 18% trimming AI (T2).
- But NO major SaaS has filed an AI-attributed seat-count decline; NRR prints are soft-not-collapsing (Asana 95% T1, HubSpot 103% guiding UP on seat expansion); consumption revenue is currently ADDITIVE (ServiceNow Now Assist $750M ACV Q1-26 with target raised to $1.5bn; Agentforce >$500M→~$1bn ARR trajectory — T1-class).
- The incumbent that re-bases survives: **ServiceNow ~50% of new ACV already non-seat** (CFO, Apr-26) with legacy SKUs killed 2026-07-01. The incumbent that stays per-seat-flat erodes.
- JP mirror: NEC BluStellar ¥705bn +30% (32% of domestic IT, margin +320bps) and Fujitsu Uvance ¥709bn +47% (target hit, record OP) = the 人月→outcome conversion executing successfully at the primes (T1 IR), while the pure man-hour/consulting cohort (Baycurrent/NRI/SHIFT/INTLOOP) got HALVED on the narrative ("Anthropic Shock").

## 2. Mechanism map — who loses, who wins, on what clock

**The capture stack, ranked (2026 evidence):**
1. **Foundation labs (direct enterprise capture)** — Anthropic run-rate $47bn May-26 (T2, unaudited), ~80% business, >1,000 customers >$1M/yr doubling in <2 months (renewal-grade); OpenAI >$25bn, enterprise >40%. The labs also invade the app surface itself (Cowork, finance-agent templates, AgentKit) = **PD-2 layer-invasion N+1: model layer invading the application layer.**
2. **Coding-vertical apps** — Cursor ~$4bn, Claude Code $2.5bn, Copilot ~$1bn. Highest token-intensity per ARR dollar; the tell: Claude Code out-earns Copilot on a smaller base because revenue scales with agentic token consumption.
3. **Regulated vertical agents** — Harvey $300M (50% of Am Law 100), Glean $300M, Sierra ~$200M: the labor-substitution ROI logic generalizes; the token intensity does not.
4. **Incumbents with usage re-basing** — ServiceNow, Salesforce(-Agentforce), Snowflake 125% NRR, Datadog ~120% NRR: capturing the transition so far, not being disintermediated.
5. **Integrators — transitional toll-booth** — Accenture: record Q2 FY26 bookings $22.1bn then Q3 −2% YoY (both T1; the derivative turned) while AI-partner bookings track to 2× = becoming a distribution channel for the labs; Globant AI ARR $20.6M vs ">$3bn pipeline"; Indian IT in "AI-led deflation."
6. **Impaired units** — per-seat-flat point tools (low switching cost) and 人月-billed SI labor (JP contract-dev core ≈ ¥8.77trn, 58% labor share, 5.5% OP margin — maximum exposure to a 10-15× hour compression).

**The dead mechanism (for now): insourcing.** Menlo T1: 76% of enterprise AI use cases BOUGHT not built — a full reversal from 2024's 47%-build; DIY succeeds ~33% vs vendor-led ~67%; model-version churn makes in-house builds a maintenance treadmill. **MIT NANDA: ~95% of pilots still deliver no measurable P&L return** (unimproved from the on-file 88%). Agent-failure data cuts AGAINST the death narrative: DIY failure funds vendors and integrators.

**The clock (three nested horizons):**
- **Now → ~2027: implementation-demand supercycle.** SIer order books at records (SCSK backlog +18.4%, NTT Data OP +51%, zero AI-attributed profit warnings from JP primes — all T1). Enterprises can't self-serve; someone must deploy the agents.

- **2029+: the re-based landscape.** The $2tn+ SaaS-cap destruction priced this event years early — a multiple story, not a 2026-27 earnings story.

**The TAM-redefinition finding (changes the whole bear case):** AI software spend is increasingly funded from HEADCOUNT budgets (Block + banks explicit, T2; ~48% of 2026 tech layoffs AI-attributed, T2/T3) — software TAM can EXPAND while seats shrink, because AI is billed against a labor pool ~10× the software pool. Counterweight: MIT — AI economically viable in only ~23% of roles today (T2).
