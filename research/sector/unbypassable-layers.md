**Test applied:** a layer is UNBYPASSABLE only if every bypass route for its binding constraint lands back INSIDE the same layer (vendor substitution allowed; layer substitution impossible absent a technological breakthrough).

## The three layers

### Layer 1 — MEMORY (stores and feeds state)

User-named; harness-confirmed at the highest signal density of any cluster (TC-1, 7-tier convergence ledger in `where-we-are.md` — supplier earnings → consumer ASP → industry → government coalition letter → insider guidance → automotive vertical → frontline supplier-side qualitative).

- Sub-tiers: **HBM** (performance rent, qualification-gated: Hynix/Samsung/Micron), **commodity DRAM/LPDDR5X** (shortage through 2027+ per coalition letter; data centers consuming 70% of memory chips produced per Tom's Hardware), **NAND** (capacity tier: SNDK held / Kioxia watch — KV-cache offload + storage overflow demand).
- **Why unbypassable:** every architectural escape route lands on another memory tier — Rubin CPX cancelled → Crescent Island LPDDR5X; NVL72 SOCAMM cut → smaller LPDDR but ASPs UP; Groq → SRAM; DeepSeek FlashMemory → NAND. Architecture-agnostic beneficiary confirmation logged 2026-06-07. The bypass routes circulate WITHIN the layer; none exit it.
- Breakthrough that would break it: compute-in-memory / radically smaller model state — none at commercial scale (P~10% within horizon, my model).

### Layer 2 — LEADING-EDGE FABRICATION CHAIN (makes the compute): litho → wafer → foundry → advanced packaging

- Chokepoints stacked in series: **ASML** EUV/High-NA literal monopoly (market-tier ratification: $674B = Europe's most valuable company ever, June 2026); **silicon wafer** duopoly-ish (Shin-Etsu + SUMCO held); **TSMC** leading-edge near-monopoly N3/N2 + **CoWoS/CoPoS** packaging (~130K wpm exit-2026, 4x vs late-2024) + ABF substrate (Ibiden) + MLCC/passives (Murata held, +182% per Rubin rack).
- **Why unbypassable:** every vendor-level bypass of NVDA (TPU, Trainium, MTIA, Tesla AI5/6, AMD) still flows through THIS layer — custom silicon changes the customer name on the TSMC order book, not the layer. Samsung/Intel foundry are intra-layer second sources, not exits. Even the memory layer's own base dies are migrating INTO this layer (HBM4E on TSMC N12/N3P — Hynix + Micron rail consolidation, 2026-06-11 verification).
- Breakthrough that would break it: nanoimprint litho (Canon), wafer-scale alternatives, mature-node analog compute — all speculative (P~5-10% within horizon, my model).

### Layer 3 — POWER & ELECTRICAL INFRASTRUCTURE (runs the compute)

- Chokepoints: grid interconnect queues 2-7 years in many ISO regions; transformer lead times >24 months; 800V HVDC transition; 600kW Rubin Ultra racks H2-2027; first hyperscaler-funded VPP (Google-Voltus 100MW, June 2026); liquid cooling ~$50K/rack (all per `bottlenecks.md`).
- **Why unbypassable:** physics — tokens are joules. The only "bypasses" are (a) efficiency, which the harness's standing Jevons-treadmill position says gets reinvested in more demand (MLA→DSA→CSA history), and (b) geography (AirTrunk India $30B/5GW = siting migration, not layer exit). Bloomberg: 50% of US 2026 DC builds delayed/cancelled — the constraint is already binding at the approval layer.
- Breakthrough that would break it: nothing breaks thermodynamics; fusion/SMR at scale merely re-vendors the layer (and is itself 2030s).

## One-liner: SK Hynix vs Kioxia/SanDisk

**Hynix sells the bandwidth the GPU cannot run without (HBM — qualification-gated, contract-priced, moat = multi-gen packaging yield); Kioxia/SanDisk sell the capacity the data center cannot store without (NAND — commodity-priced, cycle-driven, moat = scale + cost-per-bit). Same shortage, different rent: Hynix earns a durable qualification rent at the top of the memory stack; the NAND names earn a cyclical scarcity rent that is currently inflating — later-cycle, lower-moat, higher-beta echo of the same Layer-1 tightness.**

## Falsifiers for the three-layer frame

1. Compute-in-memory or sub-linear model-state breakthrough reaching commercial silicon → Layer 1 demoted from unbypassable to substitutable.
2. Nanoimprint or non-EUV leading-edge path qualified at a major foundry → Layer 2 apex (ASML) loses monopoly status.
3. Sustained efficiency gains that OUTRUN demand growth (Jevons-treadmill breaks) → Layer 3 binding pressure decays; watch tokens-per-joule trend vs total-token growth at the annual review.
