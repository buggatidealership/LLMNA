## Bottoms-up reasoning (mandatory per principle #1)

### Step 1 — Customer cohort growth model

- Q4 FY26 baseline: 733 $1M+ customers (per [SEC 8-K T1](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001640147/000162828026011631/fy2026q4earnings.htm)) averaging ~$1.67M/customer/year = ~$1.22B annualized from this cohort alone
- Q1 FY26 baseline: 604 $1M+ customers (per same source)
- Applying +27% YoY trajectory from the Q4 pattern: Q1 FY27 expected ~760-780 $1M+ customers
- Per-customer spend trajectory: ~4-6% QoQ growth in $1M+ cohort (NRR 125% ÷ 4 quarters = ~31% annualized — but Q1 is seasonally lighter than Q4)

### Step 2 — Seasonal Q4 → Q1 ratio

- Q4 FY26 product revenue: $1.23B (per SEC 8-K T1)
- Q1 FY26 product revenue: $997M (per agent research aggregating prior quarter SEC filings T1)
- Q4 FY25 product revenue: $943M (per agent research T1)
- Historical Q4 → Q1 ratio for SNOW: $997M / $943M = **1.057x** (Q1 typically 5-7% above prior Q4)
- Applying to Q4 FY26 of $1.23B: $1.23B × 1.057 = **~$1.30B implied Q1 FY27 product revenue**
- Company guided to $1.2645B midpoint — ~3% haircut from my model (consistent with L4: in contracted-demand environment with $9.77B RPO floor, mgmt sandbag is smaller than historical norms)

### Step 3 — AI workload additive (the Cortex pricing-cut wildcard)

- Cortex AI was priced 70% cheaper in April 2026 (per [T3 Medium](https://medium.com/towards-data-engineering/breaking-down-snowflakes-ai-pricing-overhaul-credits-caching-and-cost-strategy-bde56f48f53f))
- Q1 FY27 covers Feb-Apr 2026 (fiscal Q1 ends April 30) — so the April pricing change is INSIDE this quarter for ~1 of 3 months
- Short-term effect: AI revenue PER QUERY drops 70%; volume needs ~3.3x growth to be revenue-neutral
- Volume uplift typically lags pricing cuts by 2-3 quarters → Q1 AI contribution slightly NEGATIVE vs Q4 on per-dollar basis despite usage growth
- AI workload contribution to Q1: roughly net flat to Q4's ~$25M quarterly AI revenue

### Step 4 — Synthesis

- Cohort growth × consumption ramp × seasonal ratio = ~$1.30B implied
- Less ~1.5% mgmt sandbag = ~$1.275B (my base estimate)
- AI workload near-flat as offsetting forces (volume up, price down)
- Result: **$1.275B base estimate** vs $1.2645B company guide midpoint vs $1.32B implied analyst total-revenue consensus (which embeds ~$50-55M professional services)

### TARGET 2: NRR Direction (Q1 FY27)

| Scenario | NRR point | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Bear (Cortex price cut impact + Q1 seasonality) | 122% | 20% |
| **Base (slight dip from AI pricing, offset by enterprise ramp)** | **124%** | **55%** |
| Bull (AI volume surprises + large enterprise ramps) | 126% | 25% |

**Consensus expectation:** Stable at 125% (anchored on 4 consecutive quarters at 125% per agent research aggregating SEC 8-Ks).

**My base estimate is BELOW consensus** — predicting a 1pp dip to 124%. Reasoning: the April Cortex price cut is the first material new variable in the NRR equation; even with volume growth, the per-query revenue compression should net to a slight headwind in Q1.

**Bottoms-up logic for the dip:** NRR = (current-quarter revenue from prior-quarter cohort) / (prior-quarter revenue from same cohort). The April pricing cut reduces per-query AI revenue inside the current-quarter numerator while the denominator still embeds full-priced Q1 FY26 AI revenue. The math headwind is ~50-100bps of NRR.

**Falsifier for the call:**
- NRR below 120% would signal active consumption optimization + Databricks displacement underway (the bear thesis is firing)
- NRR above 127% would signal Cortex AI monetization is OUTPACING the Iceberg headwind (the bull thesis is firing)

**Non-default read (B14):** The market is watching NRR as the Iceberg-commoditization scoreboard. Even a 1pp dip to 124% with a good revenue beat could be misread as "Iceberg is winning" by bears. Framing matters as much as the number — mgmt commentary on the WHY of any NRR movement will drive stock reaction.

### TARGET 3: AI Run Rate Update (Cortex)

| Scenario | Point estimate (or behavior) | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Bear (no dollar re-quantification — re-frame to tokens/accounts) | "AI workload growth strong" — no dollar number | 30% |
| Bear-2 (dollar run rate fell below $100M due to April pricing cut) | $80-95M run rate | 15% |
| **Base (mgmt frames "growing" with new dollar number reflecting volume tailwind)** | **$110-130M run rate** | **45%** |
| Bull (volume tailwind dominates; pricing cut is working) | $150M+ run rate | 10% |

**Why this target is the highest-uncertainty one:** Cortex run rate is the most management-narrative-sensitive metric. The April price cut creates an optics dilemma:
- If mgmt reports a lower dollar run rate → headline reads "AI revenue fell"
- If mgmt reports a higher dollar run rate → confirms volume tailwind
- If mgmt re-frames to non-revenue metrics (tokens, accounts) → market reads as evasion

**Bottoms-up logic for the base $110-130M:**
- Pre-cut Q4 FY26 trajectory: ~$25M quarterly AI revenue (= $100M run rate)
- 70% pricing cut + ~1 month (April) inside Q1 = blended ~24% pricing reduction inside Q1
- Volume needs ~32% growth to offset → if volume grows 40-50% (which mgmt would have planned-for in deciding to cut prices), net AI revenue grows ~10-20%
- Result: Q1 AI revenue ~$27.5-30M = $110-130M run rate framing

**Falsifier:**
- Mgmt explicitly says AI run rate fell below $100M → structurally bearish for Cortex monetization thesis
- Mgmt says run rate exceeded $150M → very bullish; pricing cut working faster than expected
- Mgmt refuses to provide dollar figure but emphasizes account/usage growth → ambiguous (most-likely outcome if revenue went sideways)
