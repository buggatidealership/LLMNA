## Generalizable lessons

### L6 — L4's sandbag-adjustment applies MORE aggressively at the EPS/margin line than at the revenue line

**Origin**: Same pattern recurring across NVDA Q2 FY27 guide undercall ($2.5B miss per L4) AND MOD Q4 FY26 EPS undercall ($0.13-0.19 magnitude miss). In contracted-demand environments, revenue line is constrained by guide framework + bottoms-up unit economics; EPS line has compounding margin/tax/interest flow-through that the standard sandbag heuristic discounts too aggressively.

**Calibration adjustment**: For future predictions in contracted-demand environments (currently: NVDA, AVGO, TSM, NBIS, HYNIX, MU, MRVL custom Si, MOD), apply L4 smaller-sandbag-haircut to BOTH revenue (~2%) AND EPS (~3-5% reduction in sandbag haircut, more aggressive than revenue). For non-contracted environments, original sandbag still applies.

**Validation criterion**: if next ≥3 predictions show MAGNITUDE-UNDERCALL on EPS while DIRECTION correct, L6 is confirmed. If EPS predictions become consistently too aggressive, L6 needs revision.

### L7 — Management margin-recovery guides are HOPES, not contracts

**Origin**: MOD CS adj EBITDA margin guide of "+200bps recovery from Q3's 17.9%" delivered only +80bps (to 18.7%). Driver: capacity-ramp friction + tariffs + weather. These are FORESEEABLE — they were happening at Q3 already but the recovery framework didn't model them.

**Calibration adjustment**: When mgmt gives a margin-recovery guide that depends on internal execution (capacity absorption, tariff pass-through, mix shift), apply a 30-50% haircut to the guided recovery magnitude. Mgmt's optimistic guides are systematically biased toward THEIR plan, not the friction reality. Apply principle #14 (question framings) to mgmt guides, not just own framings.

**Distinction from L4**: L4 said mgmt UNDER-promises in contracted-demand environments. L7 says mgmt OVER-promises on margin-execution recovery. Both can be true simultaneously — under-promise on demand visibility, over-promise on execution friction.

### L8 — Structural-acceleration thesis on AI cooling CONFIRMED at MOD

**Positive lesson**: DC sub-segment +158% YoY at MOD validates the bypass-route framework from chip-cooling (Vertiv/Asetek/LiquidStack territory) to facility-cooling (Modine + Vertiv) per the original thesis. The $4B Airedale deal is now proven as forward-bookings visibility (not just announcement noise). Multi-quarter beat-and-raise streak confirms the cyclical-to-structural transition per principle #26.

**Implication for MOD thesis tier**: thesis was stub-Candidate (NOT held). The structural confirmation strengthens the case for upgrade to Active-candidate at 1.5-3% sizing if entered (with Stage 3 narrative-stage modifier per principle #31 — stock at +22.5% post-print and KeyBanc PT $370 means surprise capacity partially exhausted). Entry timing matters more than confirmation now.
