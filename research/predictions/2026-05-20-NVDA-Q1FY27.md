### Step 2 — Q1 FY27 supply-side constraint (the binding factor)

Demand is not the bottleneck. Hyperscaler 2026 capex = $725B (+77% YoY), bookings exceed shipments. The Q1 print is gated by **CoWoS-L packaging capacity** and **HBM3E supply** ramping at TSMC and SK Hynix respectively.

Capacity trajectory:
- TSMC CoWoS exiting calendar Q4 2025: ~55K wafers/month total, NVDA ~60% = ~33K wpm for NVDA
- TSMC CoWoS exiting calendar Q1 2026: ~65–70K wpm total, NVDA ~38–42K wpm
- Net effective NVDA CoWoS capacity growth Q4→Q1: **+18–22% q/q**
- Plus yield/throughput improvement on mature B200/B300 dies: **+3–5%**
- Net effective unit growth ceiling: **+22–27% q/q on Blackwell shipments**

Independent cross-check: industry shipment estimates put Blackwell at ~1.5M units in calendar Q4 2025 and >2M in calendar Q1 2026 — that's +33% q/q on units, consistent with capacity expansion plus inventory drawdown.

### Step 3 — ASP mix shift

GB300 NVL72 (Blackwell Ultra) ramping in Q1 FY27 at meaningfully higher ASP than GB200. System pricing for full GB300 NVL72 rack is ~$3M → ~$42K per GPU effective.

- Q4 FY26 blended system ASP: ~$40K/GPU (GB200-heavy, some HGX)
- Q1 FY27 blended ASP: ~$42K/GPU (GB300 mix climbing to ~25–30% of shipments)
- Net ASP uplift: **+4–6% q/q**

### Step 4 — Component build (point estimate)

**Data Center compute** (DC ex-networking):
- Q4 base: $52.3B
- Unit growth: +24% × ASP growth: +5% = +30% revenue growth
- Q1 FY27 compute: $52.3B × 1.30 = **$68.0B**

**Networking** (Spectrum-X, NVLink switches, BlueField):
- Q4 run-rate ~$10B
- q/q growth history: +21% (Q3→Q4 implied), then ~+20% trend
- Spectrum-X just crossed $10B annualized run-rate; attach rate per Blackwell deployment rising
- Q1 FY27 networking: $10B × 1.15 = **$11.5B** (decelerating slightly as base grows)

**Other DC** (DGX Cloud, software, services): **$1.5B**

**Total Data Center: $81.0B**

**Gaming**: Q4 was $3.7B (down -13% q/q from Q3, post-holiday digestion in gaming + GeForce Blackwell supply constrained). Q1 typically -5 to -10% seasonal. Gaming Q1 FY27: **$3.4B**

**ProViz**: ~$0.75B (slight growth, RTX Pro Blackwell ramp)
**Auto**: ~$0.65B (steady growth, ~+5% q/q)
**OEM/Other**: ~$0.15B

**Non-DC total: $4.95B**

Honest haircut: my supply-side build comes out optimistic (~$86B) because it assumes near-full conversion of CoWoS expansion into shippable units and the full GB300 ASP step-up landing inside the quarter. Reality usually has frictions — yield surprises, qualification delays, customer scheduling. I'm applying a -5% haircut for execution friction.

**Revenue point estimate: $82.0B**

Gross margin build:
- Q4 was 75.2% non-GAAP
- HBM3E cost up ~20% YoY but passed through in GB300 ASP
- Networking mix benefit (networking GM > compute GM)
- Rubin tape-out cost is opex, not COGS, this quarter
- **GM point estimate: 75.4%** (slight beat on 75% guide)

EPS build (new methodology with SBC included in non-GAAP):
- Revenue: $82.0B
- GM 75.4% → Gross profit: $61.8B
- Opex non-GAAP: $7.5B (per guide)
- Operating income: $54.3B
- Other income: ~$0.8B
- Pre-tax: $55.1B
- Tax @ 16%: -$8.8B
- Net income: $46.3B
- Diluted shares: ~24.6B
- **Non-GAAP EPS: $1.88**

### Step 6 — Q2 FY27 guide

Sequential capacity expansion from Q1 to Q2: CoWoS ramps from ~65–70K wpm to ~85–95K wpm system-wide (~+30% total capacity, NVDA share holds at 60%). Effective unit capacity growth Q1→Q2: ~+12–15% q/q (slowing as base grows). ASP uplift +2–3% (GB300 mix continuing). Net Q2 revenue trajectory: ~+13–15% q/q.

- Internal Q2 expectation: $82B × 1.13 = **$92.7B**
- NVDA's historical guide-vs-actual pattern: guides ~4–5% below the internal mid-case
- **Q2 guide point: $88.5B ± 2%** → midpoint $88.5B

### Where this differs from the aggregated v1

The bottoms-up is **more bullish on the Q1 print** than the v1 call ($82B vs $80.6B) — because the supply ramp + GB300 mix shift compounds harder than the analyst cluster modeled. Independent cross-checks (TSMC CoWoS wpm, Blackwell unit estimates, SK Hynix HBM trajectory) all point higher.

The bottoms-up is **less bullish on the Q2 guide vs whisper** than v1 ($88.5B vs $89B). Once you model the capacity-expansion smoothing, Q2 sequential growth has to *decelerate* from Q1's blowout because the supply base is bigger. Sell-side analysts who said "Q2 guide $90B" appear to be straight-lining Q1 momentum, which the supply model doesn't support.

### The differentiated trade thesis

**Print blows out vs published consensus. Guide beats consensus but UNDERSHOOTS the most aggressive whispers.**

This is a different shape than "clean beat-and-raise" — it's "blowout print, in-line-to-slightly-above guide." Stock reaction depends on whether the +$3B revenue beat dwarfs the -$1.5B guide-vs-whisper miss. My read: it should, because the print itself is unambiguously stronger than even the bull case priced in, and Jensen typically uses the call to reinforce out-quarter and FY27 H2 narrative (Rubin sampling, sovereign deals).

## What would invalidate the call

- **CoWoS yield setback**: any TSMC qualification or yield issue in Q1 caps shipments below my +24% unit assumption. Probability ~10% — no public signal of this.
- **HBM3E supply disruption**: SK Hynix or Samsung quality event. Probability ~5%.
- **China inventory charge surprise**: H20 / H200 channel mark-down. Probability ~15% but bounded ($1–3B max, well below the revenue beat).
- **GB300 ASP discipline crack**: hyperscalers negotiating harder on Blackwell Ultra pricing as second-source MI355/MI400 advance. Probability ~20%, but margin impact <50bps.
- **Macro/policy shock between now and 5pm ET**: out of model scope.

Compound probability my call is broadly right (beat across the board on print, in-line to slight beat on guide vs consensus): **~75%**.
