**🔴 CRITICAL DISAMBIGUATION on metal pitch headline:** "SMIC beats Intel 18A metal pitch" is technically accurate but structurally misleading. Intel 18A Panther Lake SHIPS at 36nm pitch because **PowerVia (backside power delivery)** moves power routing to rear of wafer, freeing front-side metal stack — making tighter pitch unnecessary. Intel 18A supports 32nm min pitch in high-density library. SemiAnalysis notes 18A has "loosest M0 pitches among leading-edge nodes due to PowerVia" as **deliberate architectural choice**, NOT a capability ceiling. SMIC achieved 32.5nm by stacking 5-6× DUV multi-patterning, at massive yield cost.

**Manufacturability ceiling (critical for TC-10 read):**
- EUV status: SMIC has **ZERO EUV access** due to export controls; N+3 uses DUV multi-patterning (5-6× passes for critical layers)
- Yield: well below EUV-typical **80-90%**; production at **operating loss**; state-subsidized
- Scope: Kirin 9030 in **ONE product** (Huawei Mate 80 Pro Max); NOT licensable to third parties under SMIC capacity constraints
- Competitive relevance: proof-of-concept sovereign node, NOT volume foundry capability. **Cannot serve HYNIX, MRVL, or any third-party fabless customer.**

**🟢 TC-10 verdict: REINFORCE WITH CEILING** — China capability progressing faster than prior consensus BUT commercial moat erosion timeline for MRVL/HYNIX/etc is **5-7 years minimum, NOT 2-3 years**.
