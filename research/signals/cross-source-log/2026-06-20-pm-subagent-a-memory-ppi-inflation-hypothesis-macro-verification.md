## PPI → CPI Propagation Analysis

| Layer | Weight in CPI All Items | Mechanism | Pass-through coefficient (historical) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Computers, peripherals, smart home assistant | 0.293% (Dec 2025 per BLS factsheet) | Direct hedonic-adjusted price input | Historically suppressed by hedonic adjustments (BLS counts spec uplift as quality improvement, not price increase) — effective pass-through often <0.5 |
| Telephone hardware (50% smartphones) | ~0.5-1.0% (sub-line of telecom services) | Direct hedonic-adjusted | Same |
| Information technology hardware + services (broader) | 1.714% (Dec 2025 per BLS) | Indirect (services dominate; hardware is ~15-20% of this bucket) | Mixed |
| **TOTAL direct electronics-PPI→CPI mechanical pass-through** | ~1.5-2.5% effective weight | n/a | Even at +25% PPI move with 0.5 pass-through coefficient = ~15-30bps macro-CPI impact |

**My model derived implication:** "Memory as inflationary headwind" framing is **structurally correct as sector narrative** (electronics OEMs eating margin OR raising prices, MediaTek/Apple/Realme explicitly confirmed) but is **mathematically marginal at macro-CPI level** (~15-30bps even at extreme magnitudes, before hedonic-adjustment further compresses). 

**Where the narrative DOES bind macro-economically:**
1. **Embedded-electronics PPI flow-through to durable goods** (autos, appliances, industrial equipment) — autos alone are ~3-4% of CPI; if memory/electronics costs propagate to vehicle MSRP, the magnitude could meaningfully compound; 2021-22 chip shortage demonstrated this channel
2. **Capex-cost-of-AI-infrastructure flow-through to enterprise software pricing** — not in CPI, but in PPI Services Final Demand
3. **Smartphone replacement-cycle disruption via OEM cost-pass-through** — Apple Cook 2026-06-17 already confirmed iPhone price hikes; if Android OEMs follow at scale, this becomes the most visible consumer-tier inflation signal

**Confidence on macro-CPI inflation-driver framing:** LOW — narrative is sector-headwind, not macro-inflation-driver in mechanical terms. Confidence on sector-OEM-margin-headwind framing: HIGH.
