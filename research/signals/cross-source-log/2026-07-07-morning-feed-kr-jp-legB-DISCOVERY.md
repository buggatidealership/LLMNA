## 2. DOT-CONNECTION (post-read; contradictions flagged loudly)

- **Portfolio/framework impact:** Not a book name, but this **INVALIDATES (negative direction)** the ambient "Korea shipbuilding+defense export supercycle is uninterruptable" narrative that KR press has been running unchecked. First hard, large-ticket loss to a NATO incumbent on **interoperability**, not price/tech. - **Harness-framework impact:** Argues for a **"NATO/alliance-bloc procurement lock-in"** modifier on any defense-export thesis — bloc membership can trump spec/price. Relevant if book ever adds KR defense/shipbuilding.
- **NEW mechanism:** Runner-up clause (TKMS talks collapse → Hanwha next) means the loss is optioned, not terminal — asymmetry worth noting.

### DOT B — "Death of SaaS → death of the SIer" (NEC/Fujitsu × Anthropic)  **[NEW-THESIS TRIGGER]**
- **Portfolio/framework impact:** No direct book name, but this is a **second-order demand-pull VALIDATE (positive)** for compute/inference: two of Japan's largest IT-services incumbents are re-platforming enterprise workloads onto Claude and putting whole workforces on it — enterprise-inference volume, not just hyperscaler training. Supports the **AI-funding-shock node's** "durable end-demand from enterprise adoption" leg (demand looks real, not purely capex-circular).
- **Harness-framework impact:** Exposes a **coverage hole** — book/harness is hardware/memory/power anchored with **no application-/services-layer disruption framework**. The value-migration (SaaS ASP → agent tokens → SIer relevance risk) is invisible to current node map.
- **NEW mechanism:** "Incumbent-embraces-disruptor-as-supplier" survival play (NEC forestalling its own disintermediation) — a template for grading which legacy IT names are anti-fragile vs terminally short.

### DOT C — Japan grid/transformer bottleneck  **[NEXT-BOTTLENECK — strong VALIDATE of TC-13]**
- **Portfolio/framework impact:** Directly **VALIDATES (positive) TC-13 power cascade**. New, concrete datapoints beyond generic "power is tight": 5–10yr *connection* waits, 3–4yr *transformer* lead times, backlogs into 2030s, Revenue-Cap (Apr-2023) as the trigger, and THREE simultaneous demand waves (AI DC + renewables interconnect + pre-1990 replacement). This is a supply-side pinch with visible order-book upside.
- **Harness-framework impact:** Promotes **grid power-equipment (large transformers / GIS / T&D)** from "TC-13 consequence" to a **standalone bottleneck-of-tomorrow candidate** with its own supply model (multi-year lead, oligopoly capacity, capex arms race quantified).
- **NEW mechanism:** **Bypass-route** — the AI power constraint is being partly routed *around* the interconnect queue via on-site/behind-the-meter generation; the transformer shortage is the harder, less-bypassable pinch (you can co-locate gas, you can't skip the step-up transformer). Trade the non-bypassable node.

### DOT D — Japan consumption -6 mo / real wages -4 yr  **[end-demand-durability caveat]**

- **Harness-framework impact:** Minor input to end-demand-durability; watch whether 2026 shuntō wage gains actually flip real wages positive (stated invalidation of the weak-consumer read).

## 4. NEW THEME / NEXT-BOTTLENECK CANDIDATES

1. **Large power transformers / grid T&D as the non-bypassable AI-power node** *(highest conviction)*
   - Mechanism: 3–4yr lead times, backlogs into 2030s, oligopoly capacity, three concurrent demand waves; step-up transformer cannot be skipped even if generation is co-located behind-the-meter.
   - Bypass-route angle: on-site gen/BTM routes *around* interconnect queues but NOT around the transformer — so the transformer is the harder pinch. Trade the node that has no bypass.

2. **Enterprise-AI displacement of the software/services layer ("SaaS死→SIer死")**
   - Mechanism: agent tokens substitute for SaaS seats and SI labor; incumbents (NEC/Fujitsu) buy their way in as disruptor-suppliers.
   - Bypass/framework angle: creates a new coverage axis the harness lacks (application/services layer); grade legacy IT names on "disruptor-supplier vs disintermediated."

3. **NATO/alliance-bloc procurement lock-in** as a defense-export ceiling
   - Mechanism: bloc interoperability (212CD) can beat spec/price; caps the KR defense-export TAM inside NATO buyers.

**What is KR/JP talking about MOST this window that the portfolio + harness has ZERO exposure/framework for?**

Two things, ranked:

1. **The application/software-services disruption layer** — the single loudest JP business-press theme this window is *日経ビジネス*'s cover "Don't get culled by AI" (NEC/Fujitsu/Salesforce/SHIFT, "death of SaaS → death of the SIer," Claude Mythos). If agent-driven substitution is real, it (a) validates enterprise-inference demand durability *and* (b) opens a whole short/long axis the OS currently cannot express. 2. **Japan's DOMESTIC grid-equipment / transformer supply chain as an investable bottleneck** (vs. treating power only as a cost input to compute). The KR/JP press treats transformers as a named, quantified, multi-year-locked shortage with an ongoing capex arms race — the harness references TC-13 power cascade but has **no dedicated transformer/T&D supply model or names**. Second-biggest gap; also the most actionable.
