## 2. Pattern extraction — §5 prior test results

**§5 prior #1 (proprietary longitudinal data): PARTIALLY FAILS the discrimination test.** Surfaces both AI-winners (HTFL CFD+registry, WAY cross-payer claims, United Imaging Chinese-patient imaging, Tempus genomic+clinical) AND non-AI winners (Guardant blood assay, ISRG surgical kinematics, GE/SHL/PHG distribution data). **Refined version: "proprietary data that the AI-specific algorithm depends on AND that the algorithm's output is reimbursed-against."**

**§5 prior #2 (regulatory or reimbursement chokepoint): CONFIRMED as the load-bearing prior.** Reimbursement chokepoint is the *binding* version of the gate — regulatory clearance alone doesn't drive revenue without reimbursement. HTFL CPT 75580, Waystar CMS-0057-F tailwind, Olympus 厚労省 +60点 are the canonical clears.

**§5 prior #3 (sell into existing budget lines): UNIVERSALLY CONFIRMED.** ZERO winners required new budget creation. HTFL replaces invasive angiography. WAY replaces RCM labor. Olympus is an add-on to existing polypectomy procedure. Olive's failure was largely about trying to create new "AI Operating System" budget category.

## 3. The framework's BIGGEST new insight: TWO distinct GREEN-pattern archetypes

The §5 priors surface **two structurally different cohorts** that the framework currently conflates. Distinguishing them is the framework's most important refinement:

| Archetype | Definition | Cohort examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Type A — Clean AI-Execution** | AI is the load-bearing moat; gate-stack clears via reimbursement-coded AI output; bypass-route resistance comes from AI-specific moat (physics, registry, cross-payer data) | **HTFL, WAY, Olympus (within EndoBRAIN segment)** |
| **Type B — Existing-Archetype + AI Overlay** | Pre-existing moat (hardware install base, assay+CLIA, distribution network, EHR-vendor lock) carries the weight; AI is feature or operational improvement | ISRG, PODD, GE/SHL/PHG, RDNT, Guardant/Exact/Natera/Veracyte, DOCS, Fujifilm, Canon, United Imaging, IQVIA, Tempus, Veeva |

**Why this matters:** Type B names are not bad — many are excellent businesses (ISRG installed base, IQVIA data spine, Veeva life-sciences vertical) — but they should be evaluated with the EXISTING archetype framework (distribution moat, device moat, network moat), NOT through the medical-AI lens. **The AI overlay is sentiment, not the investment thesis.** Type A is where the genuine medical-AI alpha lives.

## 4. The framework's SECOND new insight: layer-conditional gate applicability

The 4-gate filter applies cleanly to L5 diagnostics — the layer that surfaces both GREEN names (HTFL + Olympus). It applies UNEVENLY to other layers:

| Layer | Gate (a) Regulatory | Gate (b) Reimbursement | Gate (c) Clinical evidence | Gate (d) Distribution | Filter validity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1+L2 drug discovery | (a) is binding | (b) binary if approved | (c) is rate-limiter | (d) via pharma partnerships | Cleanly applicable |
| L3+L4 trials + mfg | (a) FDA acceptance of AI-elements (slow) | N/A — services not goods | (c) AI-trial outcome studies | (d) sponsor relationships | Partial |
| L5 diagnostics | All 4 apply | All 4 apply | All 4 apply | All 4 apply | **Cleanest fit** |
| L6 CDS | (a) software not always regulated | (b) STRUCTURALLY N/A | (c) some applies | (d) EHR integration | **2 gates structurally impossible** |
| L7 ambient docs | (a) usually N/A | (b) N/A (sold to provider) | (c) user-satisfaction not outcomes | (d) provider network | 2 of 4 N/A |
| L8 RCM/admin | (a) mostly N/A | (b) N/A | (c) quantitative cost-savings as proxy | (d) cross-payer scale | 2 of 4 N/A |

**Implication:** the framework needs **layer-specific gate substitutions** for L6/L7/L8. Recommended substitutions for the missing gates: (a) → "EHR-vendor displacement immunity"; (b) → "labor-replacement budget anchor"; with (c) measured as quantitative cost-savings disclosure.
