## §3 PRICING MODEL ANALYSIS

**Prior model:** M365 Copilot = $30/user/mo flat seat + $18 add-on. Adoption pain documented: only 35.8% of seated users active; ChatGPT chosen 76% vs Copilot 18% when offered side-by-side per [Redress T2](https://redresscompliance.com/servicenow-now-assist-ai-pricing-guide.html).

**New Cowork model:** Layered on top of M365 Copilot USL prerequisite — usage billed as Copilot Credits (product-level meter for agent actions, NOT raw tokens) at $0.01/credit. Capacity Packs $200/mo = 25k credits per [Microsoft Learn T1](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-copilot-studio/copilot-credits-overview).

**Why pivot:** Direct executive admission — agentic Cowork tasks 1000× more token-intensive than chat; "tokenmaxxing" cost crisis per [Tom's Hardware T1](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/ai-cost-crisis-hits-tech-giants-as-employee-tokenmaxxing-backfires-agentic-ai-eats-up-to-1000x-more-tokens-than-standard-ai-sparks-corporate-pullback-at-microsoft-meta-and-amazon) + [Fortune T1](https://fortune.com/2026/05/22/microsoft-ai-cost-problem-tokens-agents/) + [Axios May 28 T1](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/28/ai-spending-roi-enterprise-costs). One unnamed enterprise spent $500M/month before usage caps. Microsoft canceled most Claude Code licenses earlier this year per Suleiman over cost.

**Signal:** Seat model is breaking for agentic workloads. **This is the consensus pricing pivot for AI-agent tier.**
