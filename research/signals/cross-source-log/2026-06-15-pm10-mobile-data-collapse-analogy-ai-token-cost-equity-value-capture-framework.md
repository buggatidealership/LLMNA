## Layer-by-layer attribution of mobile-data cost collapse (verified pattern from telecom history)

| Layer | What innovated | Cost-down contribution (my model from cellular standards history) | Equity-value capture pattern |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spectrum / PHY** | 2G → 3G → LTE → 5G; OFDMA, MIMO, beamforming, mmWave | ~10× per generation × 4 generations = ~10,000× bits-per-Hz | Qualcomm captured massive royalty stream 2003-2020 BUT commoditized over time as Huawei/MediaTek/Samsung closed gap |
| **Backhaul / fiber** | DWDM density, fiber buildout (Corning, Lumen), edge fiber | ~100-1000× backhaul capacity per dollar over 17 years | Tower REITs (American Tower, Crown Castle) durable rent via geographic moats; fiber operators (Lumen, Zayo) margin pressure |
| **Network architecture / CDN** | Small cells, edge compute, CDN deployment (Akamai, Cloudflare, AWS CloudFront) | ~10-100× cost-capture via moving compute to edge | Akamai + Cloudflare captured enormous upside as cost-capture leaders — qualification-gate moat, scale economies, harder to commoditize than PHY |
| **Codec / compression** | H.264 → H.265/HEVC → AV1 → VVC; adaptive bitrate; WebP/AVIF | ~3-5× per generation = ~10-30× total | Standards bodies (MPEG-LA) royalty stream; open-source (AV1/AOMedia) captured zero direct rent |
| **Device-side compute** | Hardware video decoders, mobile GPU advancement | Enabled cost-capture at device tier | Mobile SoC vendors (Qualcomm, Apple silicon, MediaTek) |
| **Application layer (consumer-facing)** | Netflix, YouTube, Spotify, Uber, Instagram, TikTok adaptive streaming | Created DEMAND that justified the buildout | Largest equity-value capture went here — Netflix 2007 spinoff to today = 100×+ return; consumer-surplus theoretically unbounded |
| **Business model / regulatory** | Carrier unlimited plans, T-Mobile/Sprint merger, C-band auctions | ~2-5× cost-down via business-model innovation | Consolidated carriers (T-Mobile US) captured rent; pure-play tower companies durable |

## Critical historical pattern (load-bearing for AI mapping)

**Cost-down was MULTIPLICATIVE across layers, not driven by any single layer.** Total ~100,000× = ~10,000× PHY × ~100× backhaul × ~30× codec × ~10× CDN edge × business-model compounding.

**Equity-value capture distributed by MOAT TYPE, not by who innovated most:**

- 1st order (P>80%) — PHY enabler (Qualcomm) captured initial rent BUT commoditized over multi-cycle
- 2nd order (P~60%) — Infrastructure (Tower REITs, fiber operators) captured DURABLE rent via geographic/scale moats
- 3rd order (P~40%) — CDN / cost-capture middle layer (Akamai, Cloudflare) captured RESILIENT rent via qualification-gate moats
- 4th order (P~20%) — Application layer (Netflix, YouTube, TikTok) captured CONSUMER SURPLUS unbounded — biggest equity returns

## The honest analogy-driven conclusion

1. **Both Kioxia silicon-tier AND Sandisk system-tier will capture meaningful rent** during the AI cost-down trajectory — just as both Qualcomm AND Akamai captured significant equity-value upside during the mobile data collapse
2. **Long-term moat durability favors the system-tier (Sandisk) over silicon-tier (Kioxia)** because Qualcomm's commoditization risk via Huawei/MediaTek competition is the strongest historical pattern for foundational-enabler tiers over 10-15 year horizons
3. **The MOST DURABLE rent is at the production-base tier** (Yokkaichi JV is shared — like Tower REITs being shared infrastructure for all carriers; both names benefit from this rent layer)
4. **Biggest absolute returns historically went to consumer-surplus-capture application layer** (Netflix, YouTube) — in AI maps to OpenAI / Anthropic (private) and GOOG / META / MSFT (already large, harder to multi-bag)
5. **NAND-tier equity-value capture (both Kioxia AND Sandisk) likely in "Qualcomm + Akamai" range** — substantial but not multi-bagger-tier unless paired with consumer-AI volume explosion creating NEW unanticipated use cases (TikTok-equivalent for AI)

## Lateral check — where the analogy BREAKS (per LLM-native priming item 3)

Three ways the analogy doesn't fully map:

1. **Mobile data hit physics limits (Shannon's theorem, RF spectrum scarcity).** AI compute does NOT have the same hard physical limits at the algorithmic tier — better architectures (MoE, sparsity, attention efficiency) can keep improving indefinitely. **Implication:** the system-layer (Sandisk) may have MORE room to keep capturing cost-capture rent than CDN companies did, because the substrate isn't physics-bounded the same way.

2. **Mobile data demand was elastically driven by content (Netflix, YouTube).** AI demand is partly INELASTICALLY driven by enterprise productivity + sovereign-AI build-outs that don't depend on consumer adoption. **Implication:** cost-down is less critical for demand-pull because enterprise will pay even at high cost; this WEAKENS the analogy somewhat for silicon-tier urgency (enterprise customers don't switch on cost alone).

3. **NAND has different industry structure than telecom.** Mobile data was 4-carrier oligopoly per country with national-spectrum monopolies; NAND is 5-vendor global oligopoly with geographic concentration (Korea + Japan + US). **Implication:** rent-capture dynamics differ; NAND vendors may have STRONGER pricing power than carriers historically did because end-customer fragmentation is greater (every hyperscaler + sovereign-AI buildout needs NAND).
