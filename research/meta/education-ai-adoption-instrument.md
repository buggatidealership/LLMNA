## 3. WHAT IT SAYS — hyperpersonalization vs category-scaffold (evidence only)

- **Measured engagement concentrates in general assistants.** ChatGPT: ~10% of messages across 900M WAU are tutoring-like (NBER T2-independent) — even without a valid edu-WAU, the implied education message volume dwarfs every dedicated vertical's disclosed activity. Doubao (345M MAU, heavy homework use) **absorbed** its dedicated K-12 tutor app (Aixue, 7.56M MAU) in Jan 2026 — ByteDance itself collapsed the vertical into the general assistant. Pew: 54% of US teens use general-purpose chatbots for schoolwork, 59% ChatGPT brand share — teens adopted the general tool, not school-provisioned scaffolds.
- **Dedicated verticals show low activation where institutions provision them.** Khanmigo: 15% of students with access regularly use it, ~2 interactions/wk; Khan Academy is redesigning it explicitly because of low usage (T2, against-interest). CSU's 460–500K ChatGPT Edu seats: zero published usage, 52% of faculty say AI hurt teaching, renewal contested. Utah's 708K is pre-launch census. Teacher tools show huge cumulative sign-up curves (MagicSchool 7M) but the one actives snapshot implied ≤1/3 active and falling.
- **The inverse signal is unambiguous:** Chegg — a dedicated, paid, category-scaffold study product — lost ~40–50% of subscribers YoY (T1-filing) coincident with general-assistant adoption. No dedicated AI vertical shows a filed actives series growing at general-assistant rates.
- **Counter-evidence, limited:** Duolingo's 56.5M DAU proves a dedicated learning product can hold audited-class daily engagement — but the AI tier attaches at only ~9% of payers (T3, stale), so its engagement is not evidence for AI-vertical scaffolds specifically. PhysicsWallah and TAL's device (80% weekly-active, 1 hr/day) show paid dedicated products with real intensity in exam-prep markets (India/China) where stakes are high and the product bundles content + hardware, not just an AI wrapper.
- **Net read (data-bounded):** usage concentrates in general assistants used for education; dedicated scaffolds retain intensity only where they bundle proprietary content/credential/device value (Duolingo, PW, TAL), not where they wrap a general model in a classroom UI. 🟡 DIRECTIONAL — the vertical side of the ledger is mostly undisclosed, which is itself the finding.

## 5. FALSIFIER / QUALITY NOTE — what makes this decorative

1. **If it tracks REG counters, it's decorative by construction.** Cumulative registered counts are monotonic — they cannot go down and therefore carry ~zero information per observation. The instrument is only live on the 8 actives series (§2), the two inverse signals (Chegg, Kuaidui-vs-Doubao), and refresh EVENTS. If a monthly run consists of "MagicSchool now 8M sign-ups," retire it.
2. **Staleness cascade:** any series unrefreshed >2 quarters gets a STALE tag (Principle #37 convention) and stops informing the AGENTIC-DAU basket; note the audit found the original table already carried 5-year-stale (Classroom), 27-month-stale (Gauth), and 8-month-stale (ChatGPT for Teachers) numbers presented as current — the instrument's own intake must not repeat this (B60 anchored-ingest risk).
3. **Laundering re-entry:** three confirmed laundering patterns (installs→"active", downloads→"learners", goal→achievement) will recur in press coverage; every new datapoint must pass a unit check (what is the counter physically counting?) before ingest, or the instrument re-imports the corrections it exists to make.
4. **No position linkage = decorative.** Each series must map to a tradeable read: DUOL (direct), CHGG (inverse/short-thesis resolution), TAL (device bundle), COUR (registered-vs-paid gap), MSFT/GOOG (edu Copilot/Gemini disclosure events), private tracker (OpenAI/Anthropic edu conversion, Jun 2027 cliffs). If three consecutive monthly readings change no thesis file and no scenario weight, the instrument is decorative — fold it into a quarterly note and stop the monthly cadence. Re-eval anchor (Principle #45): event-anchored on the §4 decision prints; 2026-10-14 is the calendar BACKSTOP only.
5. **Single hardest falsifier of the §3 read:** any lab or teacher-vertical publishing a genuine MAU/WAU showing >30% activation of provisioned seats would break the 15%-haircut prior and the "general assistants win engagement" conclusion simultaneously — that disclosure, if it comes, outranks everything else in this file.
